I am pleased to submit the manuscript "A Precision-Controlled Null Result for a Khinchin-Signature PSLQ Family" for consideration in the Bulletin of the Australian Mathematical Society.

Summary of contribution. The paper reports a reproducible, precision-controlled null result for low-complexity integer relations among coordinates of the empirical Khinchin-signature statistic S_n evaluated on the continued-fraction partial quotients of π and of e. Across a fixed family of six 3D/4D PSLQ templates, run at 300-digit working precision with 600-digit confirmation on stability-certified prefixes, all 546 conservative attempts for π and all 1152 conservative attempts for e are unsupported; the corresponding wide-cap diagnostic reveals only high-coefficient artefacts that vanish under the conservative cap. The contribution is twofold: a disciplined, two-constant null calibration for the tested coordinate family, and a small, reproducible workflow that other authors can apply when distinguishing genuine structural absence from search-volume artefacts in PSLQ experiments. The work fits the Bulletin's experimental-number-theory readership and remains comfortably within the journal's 12-page limit (compiled length: 5 pages).
