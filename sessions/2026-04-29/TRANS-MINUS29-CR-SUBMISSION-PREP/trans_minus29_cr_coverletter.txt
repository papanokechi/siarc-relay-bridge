Title: The Trans -2/9 Identity for Degree-(2,1)
       Polynomial Continued Fractions: Empirical
       Evidence, Structural Signature, and the
       Limits of Standard Asymptotic Methods

This paper establishes a structural algebraic
identity for polynomial continued fractions in the
Trans stratum: for every degree-(2,1) integer PCF
with b_0 != b_1, the ratio a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9 holds
if and only if the characteristic root ratio equals
(13+3*sqrt(17))/4 (Theorem 4.3). This equivalence,
verified across approximately 585,000 integer
families with zero counterexamples, converts an
empirical constant into a spectral fingerprint of
the recurrence. We additionally prove that the
indicial exponent pair {1/3, 2/3} forces irrational
b_0/b_1 (Theorem 5.3), and document three standard
asymptotic frameworks that provably fail to force
the -2/9 constraint, delimiting the proof search
space for future work.
