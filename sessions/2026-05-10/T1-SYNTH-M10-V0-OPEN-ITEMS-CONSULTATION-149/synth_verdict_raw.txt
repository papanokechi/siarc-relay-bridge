### Q2 — Pattern alpha mathematical soundness

**RATIFY at HIGH for the structural claim** (h_exact is vestigial; conclusion follows from {ha_root, ha_simple} per standard Frobenius theory). **One substantive caveat.**

Direct analysis of APPENDIX A axiom block (L88–96):

The conclusion `IndicialPoly a s = fun ρ => ρ^2` is purely a property of `a` and `s`. The standard Frobenius indicial polynomial at a regular singular point of a second-order linear ODE in self-adjoint form `(a(x)y')' + c(x)y = 0` at a simple zero `s` of `a` is `ρ(ρ-1) + ρ = ρ²` (the `ρ(ρ-1)` from the leading derivative term and `+ρ` from the cross term `a'(s)·ρ`, with `c(s)y` contributing zero at order ρ-2). The hypotheses needed to derive this are:

1. `a(s) = 0` (so `s` is a singular point) — provided by `ha_root`.
2. `deriv a s ≠ 0` (so `s` is a *simple* zero; defines "apparent singularity" in this context) — provided by `ha_simple`.

The conclusion is independent of `c` entirely (the indicial polynomial of a regular singular point of `(a y')' + c y = 0` at a simple zero of `a` is determined by the order of vanishing of `a` and `a'` only, not by `c`'s value at `s`, provided `c` is regular at `s`).

The h_exact hypothesis, per APPENDIX C TASK 2 auxiliary diagnostic, reduces to `∀ x, HasDerivAt a (a x) x` (i.e., `a' = a`), which is generically false. **This is not a Frobenius hypothesis.** It appears to be either:

- A copy-paste artifact from a different lemma where `(fun x => a x · f' x)` had a non-trivial `f'`;
- Or a malformed attempt to encode "the ODE is exact" — but exactness is already a structural property of `(a·y')' + c·y` (Lemma 3 `ode_is_exact` at L75–82 establishes this for `a_coeff` directly); it does not need to be a hypothesis of the indicial-polynomial axiom.

**Caveats not visible in slot 143R substrate but visible in APPENDIX A:**

- (caveat-1) The `c` parameter in the axiom signature is **also unused in the conclusion**. The conclusion `IndicialPoly a s = fun ρ => ρ^2` does not reference `c`. This is consistent with Frobenius theory (c does not enter the indicial polynomial at this order), but means the axiom signature has *two* vestigial parameters: `c` and `h_exact`. Pattern alpha as drafted only removes `h_exact`. **Question for slot 148:** should `c` also be removed for cleanliness? My recommendation: **leave `c` in place** for slot 148 scope discipline (Pattern alpha targets `h_exact` specifically; removing `c` is a separate concern). Document `c`'s vestigial status in the axiom_reshape_report.md as a follow-up candidate (slot 150-class).

- (caveat-2) The `ha_root` hypothesis is logically independent of `h_exact` (it is consumed by Frobenius theory directly to identify `s` as a singular point). Slot 143R's analysis was correct that `h_exact` is the only redundant *named* hypothesis. The unused `c` is a parameter, not a hypothesis, and is benign.

- (caveat-3) The auxiliary lemmas at L31–84 (`hasDerivAt_a_coeff`, `b_eq_deriv_a`, `differentiable_a_coeff`, `a_coeff_pos`, `sqrt_11_sq`) and `ode_is_exact` at L75–82 establish the project's local Frobenius-precondition structure for `a_coeff`. None of these depend on `frobenius_double_root_at_apparent_singularity` itself — they are upstream foundations. **The axiom edit does not affect these.** Confirmed safe.

- (caveat-4 — substantive) **Is there a substantive author-intent reason for h_exact?** I considered: could `h_exact` have been intended as "the differential operator on the LHS of the ODE is `(a y')' + c y` with the `(a y')'` part exact"? The hypothesis as written does NOT say that — it says `a' = a`. So the answer is no; h_exact as stated is not a Frobenius hypothesis under any reasonable parsing. The author likely intended something like `∀ x, HasDerivAt (fun x => a x * f' x) (a x * f'' x + deriv a x * f' x) x` (i.e., the product rule for the ODE LHS — the content of `ode_is_exact` at L75–82), and the `(fun y => y)` partial derivative substitution was a placeholder that was never completed. **This strengthens the slot 143R analysis: not only is h_exact vestigial in the formal sense, it is also semantically incoherent as a Frobenius hypothesis.**

**Confidence elevation:** D-143-3 is RESOLVED for Pattern alpha applicability. Pattern alpha's structural claim is HIGH confidence with the c-parameter follow-up flagged. The math-content claim (Frobenius result) remains AEAL-axiom (`mathlib_gap`); that band does not change.

Multiple model perspectives on a structural argument tend to converge. Where they would diverge is on subtle elaboration/inference issues, and those are precisely what (check-3a) targets explicitly.
