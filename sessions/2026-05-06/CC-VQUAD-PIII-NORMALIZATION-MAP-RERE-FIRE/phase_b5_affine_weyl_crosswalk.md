Phase B.5 therefore performs the cross-walk between Okamoto's

## B.5.3 — Cross-walk: $W_{a}(B_{2}) \supset W((2 A_{1})^{(1)})$ (inclusion)

The Okamoto-1987 affine Weyl group $W_{a}(B_{2})$ has order $\infty$
and a finite Weyl quotient $W(B_{2})$ of order 8 (dihedral $D_{4}$).
The Sakai-side surface symmetry $W((2 A_{1})^{(1)})$ is also affine
and infinite, with finite Weyl quotient $W(A_{1} \times A_{1})$ of
order 4 (Klein four-group).

The cross-walk is at the **finite-Weyl-quotient level** an
**inclusion**:

$$W(A_{1} \times A_{1}) \;=\; \langle s_{1}, s_{2}\rangle / \langle s_{1} s_{2} = s_{2} s_{1}\rangle
   \;\subset\; W(B_{2}) \;=\; \langle s_{1}, s_{2}\rangle / \langle (s_{1} s_{2})^{4} = e\rangle,$$

with $W(A_{1} \times A_{1})$ the index-2 normal subgroup of $W(B_{2})$
generated by reflections in the long-root hyperplanes only (the
short-root reflection $s_{2}$ is the index-2 quotient generator).

In Okamoto's coordinates:

* **Long-root reflections:** $s_{1}: \mathbf{v} \to (v_{2}, v_{1})$
  and $s_{0}^{\mathrm{conj}} = s_{1} s_{0} s_{1}: \mathbf{v} \to (-1-v_{1}, -1-v_{2})$
  generate the $W(A_{1} \times A_{1})$ subgroup (commute pairwise).
* **Short-root reflection:** $s_{2}: \mathbf{v} \to (v_{1}, -v_{2})$ is
  the $\mathbb{Z}/2$ quotient generator that promotes
  $W(A_{1} \times A_{1})$ to $W(B_{2})$.

At the affine level the cross-walk extends to

$$W((2 A_{1})^{(1)}) \;\hookrightarrow\; W_{a}(B_{2})$$

as the subgroup generated by long-root affine reflections only.
This is **NOT a quotient homomorphism** in the sense the spec's
Phase B.5 sub-task B.5.3 anticipates (the spec phrasing
"quotient by the confluence-direction subgroup" presumes a
$W(D_{6}) \to W(B_{2})$ direction that, as noted in B.5.0, has no
literature anchor). It is an **inclusion** of the surface-symmetry
subgroup into Okamoto's larger ambient symmetry.
