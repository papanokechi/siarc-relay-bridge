Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript "A Complete Arithmetic Stratification
of Degree-2 Polynomial Continued Fractions: The F(2,4) Base Case"
for consideration in Mathematics of Computation.  The paper combines
exhaustive enumeration of the 531,441 degree-2 polynomial continued
fractions with integer coefficients bounded by D=4, a three-stage
PSLQ-based classification pipeline at high precision, and a rigorous
transcendence proof, to give the decomposition Rat(113,270) sqcup
Log(0) sqcup Alg(0) sqcup Trans(24) sqcup Des(400,093) = 513,387 of
the convergent subspace, together with a uniform structural identity
a_2 / b_1^2 = -2/9 across the 24 transcendental-stratum families.

I believe Mathematics of Computation is the appropriate venue
because the central contribution is computational: an algorithmic
classification of an explicit arithmetic family at certified
precision, with a machine-checkable certificate.  The paper sits
naturally in the line of recent Math. Comp. work on enumerate-and-
classify problems in number theory, including Luo and Zhou's
classification of positive definite ternary quadratic forms of level
4N (DOI 10.1090/mcom/4130), Tao, Trudgian and Yang's systematic
exploration of exponent pairs and zero-density estimates (DOI
10.1090/mcom/4138), Bellotti, Wong and Fiori's high-precision
estimates for the Riemann zeta-function argument (DOI 10.1090/mcom/
4133), Enge and Streng's computational class invariants for higher
degree CM fields (DOI 10.1090/mcom/4118), and the very recent lower
bound for Egyptian fractions of Bettin, Grenie, Molteni and Sanna
(DOI 10.1090/mcom/4190).  We argue that the F(2,4) base case
extends this line by combining enumeration, PSLQ identification,
and Lindemann-Weierstrass-grade transcendence proof in one
classification.

On computational reproducibility, we provide a full audit trail.
The manuscript anchors 35 numerical claims in an AEAL claim ledger
(supplementary file claims.jsonl), each tagged with its working
precision (dps = 300-500), the script that produced it
(f1_base_computation.py), and a SHA-256 output hash.  The
machine-checkable certificate is supplementary file
f1_base_certificate.json.  The full session record, including
intermediate verification logs, is preserved in the public SIARC
relay-bridge repository at github.com/papanokechi/siarc-relay-bridge,
and a Zenodo deposit of the preprint accompanies this submission.
