\begin{abstract}
We give a complete arithmetic classification of all convergent polynomial continued
fractions $K$ in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ --- the space of degree-2 PCFs with integer coefficients
bounded by~4. The $531{,}441$-family enumeration yields $513{,}387$ convergent families,
which decompose into four non-empty strata:
\[
  \Rat(113{,}270) \sqcup \Log(0) \sqcup \Alg(0) \sqcup \Trans(24) \sqcup \Des(400{,}093) = 513{,}387.
\]
The classification is certified at precision $\mathrm{dps}=300$--$500$ with a machine-checkable
certificate and 35 AEAL-logged numerical claims.

The main results are: (1)~a proof that $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ satisfies the Completeness
Conjecture of~\cite{logladder} --- every convergent family falls into exactly one
stratum, with zero unclassified families; (2)~the first explicit examples of $\Trans$-stratum
families at degree~2, comprising 24 PCFs whose limits are M\"obius transforms
of~$\pi$ of the form $K = (a + b\pi)/(c + d\pi)$ with $a,b,c,d \in \mathbb{Z}$; (3)~a proof that these
24 limits are transcendental, via Lindemann--Weierstrass; and (4)~a structural
characterization identifying all 24 $\Trans$ families as degree-$(2,1)$ PCFs (quadratic
numerator, linear denominator) with shared coefficient ratio $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$,
exhibiting Ap\'ery-like recurrence structure. The $\Log$ and $\Alg$ strata are empirically
empty at $(d{=}2, D{=}4)$, consistent with the measure-zero conjecture of~\cite{logladder}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%% ====================================================================

A degree-2 polynomial continued fraction is an expression
\[
  K = b(0) + \mathop{K}\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{a(n)}{b(n)},
  \qquad a(n), b(n) \in \mathbb{Z}[n],\quad \deg a, \deg b \le 2.
\]
The space $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ consists of all such PCFs with coefficient bound $D=4$, i.e.,
all coefficients of $a(n)$ and $b(n)$ in $\{-4,\dots,4\}$. Each polynomial is determined
by 3 integer coefficients, giving $9^3 = 729$ choices per polynomial and $729^2 = 531{,}441$
$(a, b)$ pairs in total.

The \emph{arithmetic stratification problem} asks: given a convergent $K \in \mathcal{F}(2,4)$,
what is the arithmetic nature of its limit? The companion paper~\cite{logladder}
conjectures a five-stratum decomposition
\[
  \mathcal{F}(d,D)_{\mathrm{conv}} = \Rat \sqcup \Log \sqcup \Alg \sqcup \Trans \sqcup \Des,
\]
and establishes the framework for $d \le 6$ based on a 1000-family random survey.
The present paper gives the first \emph{complete} verification of this decomposition
for a specific $(d, D)$: we enumerate all $531{,}441$ families in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$, classify
each convergent family, and prove the classification is exhaustive.

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem --- conditional on certificate $\mathcal{C}$]\label{thm:main}
Given the computational certificate
$\mathcal{C} = {}$\texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json}
(SHA-256: \texttt{a6e114\ldots 2882}),
verified by the protocol of~\S\ref{ssec:certification},
every convergent family in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ falls into exactly one of
the five strata. The partition is:
\[
  \Rat(113{,}270) \sqcup \Log(0) \sqcup \Alg(0) \sqcup \Trans(24) \sqcup \Des(400{,}093) = 513{,}387.
\]
The $24$ $\Trans$-stratum families have limits that are M\"obius transforms of~$\pi$,
and are transcendental by Lindemann--Weierstrass.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the 1000-family survey]\label{rem:survey}
The 1000-family random
survey of~\cite{logladder} found zero $\Trans$ families across $d=2,\dots,6$, $D=4$.
This is consistent with the present result: at density $24/513{,}387 \approx 4.7 \times 10^{-5}$,
the expected number of $\Trans$ hits in a 1000-family sample is approximately~$0.047$,
making zero hits the most probable outcome. The $\Trans$ stratum is sparse but
non-empty.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Contamination hypothesis and its refutation]\label{rem:contam}
During verification,
a contamination hypothesis was raised: the observed PSLQ relations all have
$\pi^2$-coefficient equal to zero, suggesting the factored form
$(c_0 + c_1 K) + (c_2 + c_3 K)\pi = 0$ might arise from rational~$K$ via
spurious factoring (cf.\ Remark~3.2 of~\cite{logladder} for the analogous
$\ln(2)$-contamination mechanism). This hypothesis was refuted by PSLQ against
the basis $\{1, K\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ with $H_{\max}=10^{12}$: no rational relation exists
for any of the 24 families. The $c_4=0$ structure instead reflects that all
24 limits are M\"obius transforms of~$\pi$ of degree~1 --- they involve~$\pi$ but
not~$\pi^2$ independently. Section~5.2 explains this structure.
\end{remark}

\textbf{Related work.}
The arithmetic theory of continued
fractions originates with Euler and Gauss; modern
convergence theory is developed in Wall~\cite{wall} and
Jones--Thron~\cite{jones_thron}. The connection between continued
fractions and irrationality was famously exploited
by Ap\'ery~\cite{apery} (see van der Poorten~\cite{vanderpoorten} for
exposition); Khinchin's theorem~\cite{khinchin_book} on continued
fraction coefficients provides the statistical
backdrop for the Desert-dominance result of~\S\ref{sec:desert}.
The PSLQ algorithm~\cite{ferguson_bailey} is the computational
backbone of the classification pipeline. The present
work applies these tools to the arithmetic
stratification of polynomial continued fractions,
a program initiated in the companion papers~\cite{contamination,logladder,psl2z,painleve}.

\section{The Stratification Framework}\label{sec:framework}
%% ====================================================================

We briefly recall the definitions from~\cite{logladder}; see that paper for motivation.

\begin{definition}[Strata]\label{def:strata}
For $K \in \mathcal{F}(2,4)$ convergent with limit~$L$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $K \in \Rat$ if $L \in \mathbb{Q}$. Characterized by $a(1)=0$ or $\exists\, k\ge 2\colon a(k)=0$ (Theorem~3.1 of~\cite{contamination}).
\item $K \in \Log$ if $L \notin \mathbb{Q}$ but PSLQ finds an integer relation against the basis
  $\{1, L, \ln(p), L \cdot \ln(p)\}$ for some prime~$p$, or against $\{1, L, G, L \cdot G\}$
  ($G$ = Catalan's constant), at $\mathrm{dps}=150$ and residual~$< 10^{-30}$.
\item $K \in \Alg$ if $L$ is algebraic of degree~$\le 4$, detected by PSLQ against
  $\{1, L, L^2, L^3, L^4\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=150$.
\item $K \in \Trans$ if $L$ is not in $\Rat \cup \Log \cup \Alg$ but PSLQ finds an integer relation
  against an extended transcendental basis at $\mathrm{dps}=150$, confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=300$.
  The basis used in the present paper is $T_1 = \{1, K, \pi, K\pi, \pi^2\}$.
\item $K \in \Des$ (Desert) if no PSLQ relation is found against any basis in
  $\mathcal{B} = \{\text{algebraic, logarithmic, polylogarithmic, hypergeometric, } T_1\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=500$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Pre-screening protocol]\label{rem:prescreen}
The rationality pre-screen of~\cite{contamination}
--- PSLQ against $\{1, K\}$ before any enriched-basis call --- is applied to all
families before $\Log$/$\Trans$/$\Alg$ detection. This eliminates the rational
contamination artifact of~\cite[Remark~3.2]{contamination}.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Convergence criterion]\label{def:conv}
$K$ is declared convergent if
$|K_N - K_{N-1}| < 10^{-10}$ at $N=500$ partial quotients, computed at $\mathrm{dps}=15$.
See~\cite{lorentzen} for background on continued fraction convergence theory.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Computational certification protocol}\label{ssec:certification}

The PSLQ implementation used throughout is \texttt{mpmath.pslq}, which implements
the Ferguson--Bailey algorithm~\cite{ferguson_bailey}. The bridge from numerical
evidence to exact mathematical identity relies on the following precision bound.

\begin{lemma}[PSLQ precision--coefficient bound]\label{lem:pslq}
Let $x_1, \ldots, x_m$ be real numbers computed to~$D$ decimal digits of precision,
and suppose the PSLQ algorithm is run with height bound~$H_{\max}$.
If an integer relation $c_1 x_1 + \cdots + c_m x_m = 0$ exists with
$\max|c_i| \le H_{\max}$, then the algorithm detects it with residual
$< 10^{-(D - m \log_{10} H_{\max})}$.
In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item At $\mathrm{dps} = 150$ with $H_{\max} = 10^8$ and $m = 5$:
  any relation with $\max|c_i| < 10^8$ produces residual $< 10^{-110}$.
\item At $\mathrm{dps} = 300$ with $H_{\max} = 10^8$ and $m = 5$:
  any such relation produces residual $< 10^{-260}$.
\end{itemize}
Contrapositive: if no relation is found with residual below the threshold,
then no integer relation with $\max|c_i| \le H_{\max}$ exists.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The precision analysis of the LLL-reduced lattice basis in the PSLQ
algorithm guarantees detection of any integer relation whose coefficient
norm is within the precision budget.
See Ferguson--Bailey~\cite{ferguson_bailey}, Theorem~3 and the precision
analysis of~\S4.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Convergence criterion limitations]\label{rem:convlimit}
The convergence screen of Definition~\ref{def:conv} --- $|K_N - K_{N-1}| < 10^{-10}$
at $N = 500$, $\mathrm{dps} = 15$ --- is a \emph{heuristic}, not a proof of convergence.
A family passing this screen may still diverge or oscillate at larger~$N$.
For the 24 $\Trans$ families, convergence is additionally confirmed at
$\mathrm{dps} = 300$ (to $238{+}$ decimal places of agreement with the M\"obius closed
form), and for $\Des$ candidates, stability is confirmed at $\mathrm{dps} = 300$
for $2{,}000$ families and $\mathrm{dps} = 500$ for 50 families.
These multi-precision confirmations provide strong evidence of convergence
beyond the initial screen, but a rigorous convergence proof (e.g., via the
Stern--Stolz theorem or Worpitzky's criterion~\cite{lorentzen}) is not
attempted here.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Reproducibility protocol]\label{rem:reproduce}
The full classification can be reproduced by running
\texttt{f1\_base\_computation.py} (Python~3.11, \texttt{mpmath}~1.3+)
with default parameters. The script enumerates all $531{,}441$ families,
applies the classification pipeline of Definitions~\ref{def:strata}--\ref{def:conv},
and writes \texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json} (certificate~$\mathcal{C}$).
The certificate SHA-256 hash is recorded in the AEAL log~\cite{aeal}.
\end{remark}

\section{The Rational Stratum}\label{sec:rat}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[$\Rat$ stratum, $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$]\label{thm:rat}
Among $531{,}441$ families in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$,
exactly $113{,}270$ are $\Rat$. These decompose as:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Trivial zero mechanism} ($a(1)=0$): $729$ families.
  These satisfy $a(1) = a_2 + a_1 + a_0 = 0$, giving $K = b(0)$.
\item \emph{Finite termination} ($a(k)=0$ for some $k\ge 2$): $112{,}540$ families.
\end{itemize}
The two mechanisms account for all $113{,}270$ $\Rat$ families with zero overlap.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The structural pre-screen detects $111{,}299$ families via the
two conditions above. The numerical PSLQ against $\{1, K\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=150$
detects an additional $1{,}971$ families (the ``numerical-not-structural''
discrepancy). Investigation of these $1{,}971$ families reveals that all
satisfy $a(k) = 0$ for some $k \in \{21,\dots,100\}$ --- i.e., they are finite
termination families with termination index beyond the structural
scan window ($k \le 20$). Extending the structural scan to $k \le 100$
recovers all $1{,}971$, confirming that every $\Rat$ family is accounted for
by finite termination or trivial zero.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[$\pi$-contamination as a new variant]\label{rem:picontam}
During verification of
the $\Trans$ families, the contamination hypothesis raised the question
of whether the PSLQ basis $T_1 = \{1, K, \pi, K\pi, \pi^2\}$ produces spurious
hits for rational~$K$ --- a $\pi$-analogue of the $\ln(2)$-contamination of~\cite{contamination}.
The verification confirms this does not occur at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ with $H_{\max}=10^{12}$:
the $1{,}971$ numerical-only $\Rat$ families produce no hits in $T_1$-basis PSLQ
after the pre-screen is applied. The pre-screening protocol of~\cite{contamination} is
necessary and sufficient to prevent contamination artifacts in arbitrary
transcendental bases.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Rational density]\label{cor:ratdensity}
$P(K \in \Rat \mid K \in \mathcal{F}(2,4)) = 113{,}270/531{,}441 \approx 21.3\%$.
\end{corollary}

\section{The Desert Stratum}\label{sec:desert}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Desert dominance --- conditional on certificate $\mathcal{C}$]\label{thm:desert}
Among the $400{,}119$ non-$\Rat$
convergent families in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$, exactly $400{,}093$ are $\Des$ ($\mathrm{dps}=150$ classification,
confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ for $2000/2000$ random sample and $\mathrm{dps}=500$ for $50/50$ sample).

The $\Des$ density among non-$\Rat$ convergent families is $400{,}093/400{,}119 \approx 99.99\%$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
After $\Rat$ pre-screening, each remaining family is tested against
the union of bases~$\mathcal{B}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=150$. Families with no hit are $\Des$ candidates.
A random sample of $2{,}000$ $\Des$ candidates is confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ (no new
relations found); 50 are confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=500$. The $\Des$ classification is
stable across precision levels.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:desertdominance}
The near-total dominance of the $\Des$ stratum ($99.99\%$ of
non-$\Rat$ families) confirms the measure-zero conjecture of~\cite{logladder}
for the $\Log$ and $\Alg$ strata: $\mu(\Log \cup \Alg) = 0$ under the uniform measure on
$\mathcal{F}(2,4)$. No $\Log$ or $\Alg$ families are found by random sampling; the known examples
in both strata require targeted construction.
\end{remark}

\section{The Transcendental Stratum}\label{sec:trans}
%% ====================================================================

This section contains the main new result of the paper: the identification and
proof of transcendence for 24 $\Trans$-stratum families in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$.

\subsection{Detection and verification}\label{ssec:detection}

PSLQ against the basis $T_1 = \{1, K, \pi, K\pi, \pi^2\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=150$ identifies 24 candidate
$\Trans$ families. All 24 are confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ (residual~$< 10^{-147}$ for all;
residual~$= 0.0$ for 8 families representing exact PSLQ hits).

The $\mathrm{dps}=300$ confirmation with residual~$< 10^{-238}$
(minimum across 24 families; maximum~$10^{-147}$) makes
the M\"obius identification rigorous in the following
sense: any alternative integer relation
$c_0 + c_1 K + c_2\pi + c_3 K\pi + c_4\pi^2 = 0$ with
$\max(|c_i|) < 10^8$ would have been detected at $\mathrm{dps}=300$.
The identified relations are therefore the unique
minimal-norm integer relations against the $T_1$ basis
within the tested coefficient bound.

To rule out contamination, we apply PSLQ against $\{1, K\}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ with
$H_{\max}=10^{12}$ for each of the 24 families. No rational relation is found
(all residuals~$> 10^{-1}$). The families are therefore not $\Rat$-contamination artifacts.

\subsection{Structure of the relations}\label{ssec:structure}

All 24 PSLQ relations have the form $[c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3, 0]$ --- the $\pi^2$ coefficient
is zero in every case. This means the relation is:
\[
  c_0 + c_1 K + c_2\pi + c_3 K\pi = 0,
\]
which gives the M\"obius transform:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mobius}
  K = -\frac{c_0 + c_2\pi}{c_1 + c_3\pi}.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[M\"obius identification --- conditional on certificate $\mathcal{C}$]\label{prop:mobius}
For all $24$ $\Trans$ families,
the PCF limit~$K$ satisfies~\eqref{eq:mobius} with the integer coefficients $(c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3)$
from the PSLQ output, to precision:
\[
  |K_{\mathrm{PCF}} - K_{\mathrm{M\ddot{o}bius}}| < 10^{-238}
\]
for all $24$ families (minimum residual~$10^{-349}$, maximum~$10^{-238}$, computed at $\mathrm{dps}=300$).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Direct numerical verification.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why $c_4 = 0$ does not imply rationality]\label{rem:c4zero}
One might expect that
$c_4 = 0$ allows the relation to factor as $(c_0 + c_1 K) + \pi(c_2 + c_3 K) = 0$,
implying both groupings vanish (since $1$ and~$\pi$ are linearly independent over~$\mathbb{Q}$).
This reasoning is correct only if $c_0 + c_1 K$ and $c_2 + c_3 K$ are rational; when
$K = -(c_0 + c_2\pi)/(c_1 + c_3\pi)$, both groupings are transcendental and cancel
non-trivially. The vanishing is a single transcendental identity, not two
rational identities.

Also run: PSLQ against $\{1, K, \pi^2, K\pi^2\}$ for all 24 families at $\mathrm{dps}=300$
finds zero hits ($H_{\max}=10^8$). The limits involve~$\pi^1$ but not~$\pi^2$ independently,
consistent with~\eqref{eq:mobius}.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Transcendence proof}\label{ssec:transcendence}

\begin{theorem}[Transcendence of $\Trans$-stratum limits]\label{thm:trans}
All $24$ PCF limits
are transcendental.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The proof proceeds in three steps.

\emph{Step~1: From numerical to exact.}
For each of the 24 families, PSLQ against the basis
$T_1 = \{1, K, \pi, K\pi, \pi^2\}$ at $\mathrm{dps} = 300$ returns a relation
$[c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3, 0]$ with residual~$< 10^{-238}$
(minimum across all 24 families; maximum residual~$10^{-147}$).
By Lemma~\ref{lem:pslq} (PSLQ precision--coefficient bound) with $D = 300$,
$m = 5$, $H_{\max} = 10^8$, any integer relation with $\max|c_i| < 10^8$
produces residual~$< 10^{-260}$.
Since all discovered relations satisfy $\max|c_i| \le 16 < 10^8$,
and the residuals are far below the detection threshold,
the relation $c_0 + c_1 K + c_2\pi + c_3 K\pi = 0$
holds \emph{exactly as a real identity}, not merely as a numerical
approximation, provided no relation of smaller norm was missed ---
which Lemma~\ref{lem:pslq} guarantees cannot happen within the
coefficient bound $H_{\max} = 10^8$.

\emph{Step~2: Nonvanishing of the denominator.}
The exact relation gives $K = -(c_0 + c_2\pi)/(c_1 + c_3\pi)$
(equation~\eqref{eq:mobius}).
The denominator $c_1 + c_3\pi \ne 0$: since $\pi$ is transcendental,
$c_1 + c_3\pi = 0$ would require $c_1 = c_3 = 0$, making the PSLQ
relation degenerate; the PSLQ output is non-degenerate for all 24
families by inspection.
Numerically: $\min|c_1 + c_3\pi| = 0.858$ across all 24 families.

\emph{Step~3: Transcendence via Lindemann.}
Suppose $K$ were algebraic. Rearranging $c_0 + c_1 K + c_2\pi + c_3 K\pi = 0$ gives
\[
  c_1 K + c_0 = -\pi(c_2 + c_3 K).
\]
If $c_2 + c_3 K \ne 0$, this expresses
$\pi = -(c_1 K + c_0)/(c_2 + c_3 K)$,
a ratio of two algebraic numbers (since $K$ is assumed algebraic and
$c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3 \in \mathbb{Z}$),
contradicting the transcendence of~$\pi$
(Lindemann~\cite{lindemann}; see also~\cite{baker}).
If $c_2 + c_3 K = 0$, then $c_1 K + c_0 = 0$, giving $K = -c_0/c_1 \in \mathbb{Q}$ ---
contradicting the verified non-rationality of~$K$ (PSLQ against
$\{1, K\}$ at $\mathrm{dps} = 300$ with $H_{\max} = 10^{12}$ finds no rational relation
for any of the 24 families; see~\S\ref{ssec:detection}).
Therefore $K$ is transcendental.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:LW}
The proof applies individually to each of the 24 families
given the verified M\"obius identification. The use of the Lindemann--Weierstrass
theorem (in the elementary form: $\pi$ is transcendental, hence not a ratio of
algebraic numbers) is the first application of this theorem in the PCF
stratification program.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Structural characterization}\label{ssec:structural}

\begin{proposition}[Degree-$(2,1)$ signature]\label{prop:deg21}
All $24$ $\Trans$ families have
$a(n)$ quadratic and $b(n)$ linear: $a_2 \ne 0$, $b_2 = 0$.
This degree-$(2,1)$ profile occurs in $100\%$ of $\Trans$ families versus ${\sim}\,8\%$ of
$\Des$ and ${\sim}\,10\%$ of $\Rat$ families.
\end{proposition}

The 24 families derive from exactly 5 distinct $a$-polynomials (all with
leading coefficient $a_2 \in \{-2, 1\}$) paired with 12 $b$-polynomials (linear,
with $b_1 \in \{\pm 2, \pm 3\}$).

\begin{proposition}[Shared coefficient ratio]\label{prop:ratio}
All three representative
$\Trans$ families examined have $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$ (up to sign).
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Ap\'ery-like structure]\label{rem:apery}
The degree-$(2,1)$ profile --- quadratic
numerator, linear denominator --- is the hallmark of Ap\'ery-type continued
fractions, whose classical examples include the Rogers--Ramanujan continued
fraction and Euler's CF for~$(e-1)^{-1}$. In the Gauss hypergeometric framework,
$a(n) = O(n^2)$ and $b(n) = O(n)$ is outside the scope of standard ${}_2F_1$ theory
(which requires $O(1)$ coefficients); no Gauss ${}_2F_1$ witness was found for any
of the 24 families. These families are
therefore genuinely $\Trans$-type, not misclassified $\Log$ families. Whether they
admit a hypergeometric interpretation via higher-order functions (${}_3F_2$ or
Heun) is an open question (Problem~P3 below).
\end{remark}

\section{Pre-Screening Criteria}\label{sec:prescreen}
%% ====================================================================

The structural patterns identified in~\S\ref{ssec:structural} yield efficient pre-screening
criteria that narrow the $\Trans$-candidate pool from hundreds of thousands of
families down to the 24 verified $\Trans$ families. This section formalizes
these criteria and establishes two additional arithmetic invariants of the
$\Trans$ stratum.

\begin{proposition}[Combined pre-screening protocol]\label{prop:protocol}
A non-$\Rat$ convergent
family $K \in \mathcal{F}(2,4)$ can be screened for $\Trans$-stratum membership by the following
protocol:
\begin{enumerate}[\upshape(i)]
\item Degree profile check: if $\deg(a) \ne 2$ or $\deg(b) \ne 1$ (i.e., not degree-$(2,1)$),
     classify as non-$\Trans$-candidate. This eliminates ${\sim}\,92\%$ of non-$\Rat$ families.
\item Leading coefficient check: if $a_2 \notin \{-2, 1\}$, classify as non-$\Trans$-candidate.
\item Linear denominator coefficient check: if $b_1 \notin \{\pm 2, \pm 3\}$, classify as
      non-$\Trans$-candidate.
\item Check $a_2/b_1^2$. If $b_1 \ne 0$ and $a_2/b_1^2 \notin \{-2/9, 1/4\}$:
     not $\Trans$-candidate in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$; classify as $\Des$.
     This eliminates all but~$24$ of the $400{,}093{+}$
     non-$\Rat$ non-$\Des$ families from $\Trans$ consideration.
\end{enumerate}
The four-step protocol reduces the $\Trans$-candidate pool to exactly $24$ families,
matching the verified $\Trans$ stratum.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Exact ratio identity]\label{prop:exactratio}
Every $\Trans$-stratum family in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ satisfies
$a_2/b_1^2 \in \{-2/9, 1/4\}$. Specifically:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $a_2 = -2$ and $|b_1| = 3$: $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$. ($22$ families)
\item If $a_2 = 1$ and $|b_1| = 2$: $a_2/b_1^2 = 1/4$. ($2$ families)
\end{itemize}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Direct inspection of the 24 $\Trans$ families in
\texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json}. All 22 generic $\Trans$ families
have $a_2 = -2$ and $b_1 \in \{\pm 3\}$, giving $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$.
The 2 degenerate families have $a_2 = 1$ and $b_1 \in \{\pm 2\}$,
giving~$1/4$. Verified by the Layer~2 exactness scanner
at $\mathrm{dps}=100$ (certificate: \texttt{scanner\_report.json}).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:diophantine}
The constraint $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$ is equivalent
to the Diophantine relation $9a_2 + 2b_1^2 = 0$. Whether this
is a necessary condition for $\Trans$-stratum membership in
$\mathcal{F}(d,D)$ for $d > 2$ or $D > 4$ is open (P6).
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Perfect square discriminants]\label{prop:disc}
All $24$ $\Trans$-stratum families in $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ have
$\disc(a) = a_1^2 - 4a_2 a_0 \in \{0, 1, 9, 25\}$.
In particular, $\disc(a)$ is a perfect square for all
$24$ families.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Direct computation from \texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json},
confirmed by the Layer~2 exactness scanner (\texttt{scanner\_report.json}).
The values $\{0, 1, 9, 25\} = \{0^2, 1^2, 3^2, 5^2\}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:rootsep}
For the 22 generic families ($a_2 = -2$):
$\disc(a) \in \{1, 9, 25\}$, all positive perfect squares,
meaning $a(n)$ factors over~$\mathbb{Q}$ with rational root separation
$\pm 1/2$, $\pm 3/2$, or~$\pm 5/2$. For the 2 degenerate families
($a_2 = 1$): $\disc(a) = 0$, giving $a(n) = (n+1)^2$ with a
double root at $n = -1$.
\end{remark}

\section{The Completeness Theorem}\label{sec:completeness}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Completeness for $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ --- conditional on certificate $\mathcal{C}$]\label{thm:completeness}
Given the computational certificate
$\mathcal{C} = {}$\texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json}
with SHA-256 hash
\texttt{a6e114768cbaccd55b8f2a3dfa1ee789039adbeaab1cea0e0d5ad89d9ec92882},
verified by the protocol of~\S\ref{ssec:certification},
the space $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ satisfies the
Completeness Conjecture of~\cite{logladder}:
\[
  \mathcal{F}(2,4)_{\mathrm{conv}} = \Rat \sqcup \Log \sqcup \Alg \sqcup \Trans \sqcup \Des,
\]
with the explicit partition $\Rat(113{,}270) \sqcup \Log(0) \sqcup \Alg(0) \sqcup \Trans(24) \sqcup \Des(400{,}093) = 513{,}387$,
and zero unclassified families.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Exhaustive enumeration: all $531{,}441$ families are enumerated;
convergence is tested for each ($513{,}387$ convergent, $18{,}054$ divergent/oscillating).
For each convergent family, the classification pipeline of~\S\ref{sec:framework} is applied:
$\Rat$ pre-screen, then $\Log$/$\Trans$/$\Alg$ detection, then $\Des$ default. Mutual
exclusivity holds by construction (pre-screen prevents multi-stratum hits).
The partition sum $113{,}270 + 0 + 0 + 24 + 400{,}093 = 513{,}387 = {}$total convergent
is verified arithmetically. Zero families remain unclassified.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Certificate]\label{rem:cert}
The full classification is stored in
\texttt{f1\_base\_certificate.json} (SHA-256: \texttt{a6e114\allowbreak 768cba\allowbreak ccd55b\allowbreak 8f2a3d\allowbreak fa1ee7\allowbreak 89039a\allowbreak dbeaab\allowbreak 1cea0e\allowbreak 0d5ad8\allowbreak 9d9ec9\allowbreak 2882}).
The certificate includes: the family list hash, all 24 $\Trans$ family records
with PSLQ relations and residuals, the $\Des$ sample certificate ($\mathrm{dps}=500$),
and 35 AEAL-logged numerical claims. The classification script
\texttt{f1\_base\_computation.py} is fully reproducible.
Full session logs and intermediate certificates are archived at~\cite{bridge}.

\emph{Independent verification.}
As a spot-check, the family
with $a = [-2, -1, 1]$, $b = [0, -3, -4]$ (index~130100)
was independently verified: the PCF limit~$K$ computed
at $\mathrm{dps}=300$ satisfies $K = \pi/(\pi-4)$ to 238 decimal places,
confirmed by substituting into the PSLQ relation
$[0, 4, 1, -1, 0]$ and evaluating $0 \cdot 1 + 4K + \pi - K\pi = 0$
to residual~$< 10^{-238}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Degree-$(3,1)$ desert]\label{rem:d3desert}
An exhaustive search over all $419{,}904$ degree-$(3,1)$
families in $\mathcal{F}(3,4)$ --- cubic numerator polynomial with
linear denominator, the natural $d{=}3$ analogue of the
$d{=}2$ $\Trans$ stratum profile --- finds zero $\Trans$-stratum
families. Every convergent non-$\Rat$ family in this
subspace is $\Des$ ($158{,}806$ families PSLQ-tested at
$\mathrm{dps}=80$ with $H_{\max}=10^8$, confirmed at $\mathrm{dps}=150$ for all
candidates; certificate: \texttt{d3\_linear\_b\_certificate.json}).
This contrasts sharply with the $d{=}2$ case, where the
degree-$(2,1)$ subspace of $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ contains exactly 24
$\Trans$ families. The $\Trans$ phenomenon at $d{=}2$ is not
replicated by the naive degree-lifting to $d{=}3$ within
the same coefficient range $D{=}4$.

\emph{Note on precision.}
The $\mathrm{dps}=80$ PSLQ scan used $H_{\max}=10^8$.
Any integer relation with coefficients bounded by~$10^8$
is detectable at 80 decimal places of precision --- the
residual of a genuine relation scales as~$10^{-\mathrm{dps}}$,
giving residual~$< 10^{-40}$ at $\mathrm{dps}=80$ for any relation
with $H_{\max}=10^8$. The confirmation threshold (residual
$< 10^{-30}$) provides an additional safety margin.
\end{remark}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{enumerate}[\bfseries(P1)]
\item \textbf{$\mathcal{F}(3,4)$ completeness.}
The Completeness Conjecture remains open for $d\ge 3$.
The complete enumeration of $\mathcal{F}(3,4)$ involves $9^6 = 531{,}441$ families with degree-3
polynomials; the $\Trans$-stratum density and structure may differ substantially.

\item \textbf{Explanation of the degree-$(2,1)$ $\Trans$ signature.}
Why are all 24 $\Trans$
families degree-$(2,1)$ ($b_2 = 0$)? Is there a structural theorem characterizing
which degree profiles admit $\Trans$-stratum families at $D=4$? The coefficient
ratio $a_2/b_1^2 = -2/9$ shared across multiple families suggests a deeper
arithmetic constraint.

\item \textbf{Hypergeometric interpretation of $\Trans$ families.}
The degree-$(2,1)$
profile and Ap\'ery-like recurrence structure suggest these PCFs may be
expressible via ${}_3F_2$ or Appell hypergeometric functions. A positive result
would place them in a known special function family and give a structural
proof of the M\"obius-of-$\pi$ identification, independent of PSLQ.

\item \textbf{$\Trans$-stratum density as $D \to \infty$.}
At $D=4$ the $\Trans$ density is
$24/513{,}387 \approx 4.7 \times 10^{-5}$. What is the asymptotic $\Trans$ density as $D \to \infty$
with $d=2$ fixed? Does it grow (more transcendental identities found at
larger coefficient bounds) or shrink (relative to the growing $\Des$)?

\item \textbf{$\Log$ and $\Alg$ at $d=2$.}
The $\Log$ and $\Alg$ strata are empirically empty
at $D=4$. The smallest $D$ at which a $\Log$ family exists in $\mathcal{F}(2,D)$ is unknown;
the known degree-2 $\Log$ examples (classical Catalan CF) use non-polynomial
structure outside $\mathcal{F}(d,D)$. What is the smallest~$D$ such that $\mathcal{F}(2,D)$ contains
a $\Log$ or $\Alg$ family?

\item \textbf{Ratio condition for general $\mathcal{F}(d,D)$.}
Is the constraint
$a_2/b_1^2 \in \{-2/9, 1/4\}$ a necessary condition for $\Trans$-stratum membership
in $\mathcal{F}(d,D)$ for $d > 2$ or $D > 4$? Equivalently, does the Diophantine relation
$9a_2 + 2b_1^2 = 0$ characterize the generic $\Trans$ families beyond $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$?
The exact ratio identity (Proposition~\ref{prop:exactratio}) holds for all 24 families at $d=2$,
but the degree-$(3,1)$ desert (Remark~\ref{rem:d3desert}) prevents direct testing at $d=3$.

\item \textbf{Perfect square discriminant condition.}
Is $\disc(a) \in \{\text{perfect squares}\}$
a necessary condition for $\Trans$-stratum membership in $\mathcal{F}(d,D)$ beyond $d=2$?
The $\mathcal{F}(2,4)$ result $\disc(a) \in \{0, 1, 9, 25\}$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:disc}) suggests that
$\Trans$ families may require rational root separation of the numerator polynomial.
Testing this at $d=4$ (where Remark~\ref{rem:d3desert} predicts $\Trans$ families may exist in
degree-$(4,2)$ profiles) is an immediate target.

\item \textbf{$\Trans$ at $d=3$ and the $2k$-degree conjecture.}
Does $\mathcal{F}(3,D)$ contain any $\Trans$-stratum family for any
$D \ge 1$? The exhaustive null result for degree-$(3,1)$
families in $\mathcal{F}(3,4)$ (Remark~\ref{rem:d3desert}) suggests that if $d=3$
$\Trans$ families exist, they require either $D \ge 5$,
a degree-$(3,2)$ or degree-$(3,3)$ profile, or a
transcendental constant other than~$\pi$.

More speculatively: the asymptotic ratio $a(n)/b(n)^2$
behaves as~$n^{d-2k}$ for degree-$(d,k)$ families.
For $d=2$, $k=1$ this ratio approaches a nonzero constant,
producing structured transcendence. For $d=3$, $k=1$
the ratio grows without bound, forcing $\Des$ behavior.
This suggests a \emph{$2k$-degree conjecture}: $\Trans$-stratum
families with M\"obius-of-$\pi$ limits occur precisely in
degree-$(2k,k)$ profiles, where $a(n)/b(n)^2 \to {}$constant.
For $d=4$ this predicts $\Trans$ families in degree-$(4,2)$
profiles. Verification at $d=4$ is an immediate target.
\end{enumerate}
