Dear Professor Zudilin,

I am writing to request your endorsement for an arXiv submission
to the `math.NT` category, with cross-listing to `math.CA`.

**Paper summary.** We present a transcendence predicate for
degree-two polynomial continued fractions (PCFs), based on the
sign of the balanced discriminant Δ = β² − 4αγ of the
denominator polynomial b_n = α n² + β n + γ. Working inside the
Spec(K) classification framework, we describe a v5 upgrade of
the schema that adds the Stokes exponent and the connection-
coefficient proxy as components 6 and 7. Our central empirical
contribution is a sharp dichotomy across thirty degree-two
families: twenty-four families in the F(2,4) Trans-stratum
have Δ ∈ {+1, +8} and admit elementary closed forms, while six
candidates with Δ ∈ {−3, −4, −7, −11, −20, −28} all return
PSLQ non-detection against an eighteen-element constants basis
at working precision 220 digits and integer bound 10¹⁰. We
record these as Conjecture A v5 (parts (i)–(iv)). The
Painlevé III(D₆) transcendental nature of the Δ = −11
prototype V_quad is recalled, and a Stokes-exponent diagnostic
is introduced and verified across all six Δ < 0 degree-two
families, including intra-CM-field replication using QL06 and
QL26 (both lying in ℚ(√−7)).

**Mathematical pedigree and relevance to your work.**  The
paper sits squarely in the modern continued-fraction /
irrationality-measure tradition: Apéry (1979) for ζ(3),
Rivoal–Zudilin (early 2000s) for odd-zeta-value irrationality,
and the Cohen–Zudilin 2025 work on continued fractions and
irrationality measures for Chowla–Selberg gamma quotients
(arXiv:2510.00215).  PCF-1 v1.3 develops a Spec(K) invariant
schema in which the six Δ < 0 PCFs from the Ramanujan-Machine
catalogue serve as a test set for transcendence predicates in
the spirit of Schneider, Chudnovsky, and Wolfart–Wüstholz; the
Stokes-exponent diagnostic that distinguishes the six is the
new ingredient.
