**Subject:** arXiv endorsement request for `math.CA` submission "Channel theory for polynomial continued fractions: asymptotic channels, the ξ₀ = 2/√β₂ identity, and a bridge conjecture"

Dear Professor Zudilin,

I am writing to request your endorsement for an arXiv submission
to the `math.CA` category, with cross-listing to `math.NT`.

**Paper summary.**  We propose, define, and catalogue asymptotic
channels for sequences arising from polynomial continued
fractions (PCFs).  Each channel is a triple (D, T, S)
specifying a formal-series space D, an asymptotic gauge T, and
an analytic-continuation section S; three concrete channels
appear in the SIARC stack — the recurrence-parameter channel
L(t), the Borel-of-trans-series channel BoT, and the
connection-coefficient channel CC.  The keystone result of
v1.3 is a Newton-polygon proof that for every degree-2 PCF
(1, b) in scope with b(n) = β₂ n² + β₁ n + β₀ the leading
Borel-plane singularity of the generating function
f(z) = Σ Q_n z^n sits at radius ξ₀ = 2/√β₂, with secondary
exponent ρ = −3/2 − β₁/β₂.  v1.2 corrected an earlier
candidate c(d) = 2√((d−1)!) to the linear form
ξ₀(b) = d / β_d^(1/d), verified at d = 4 (PCF2-SESSION-Q1,
spread 0).

**Mathematical pedigree and relevance to your work.**  CT v1.3
sits at the intersection of continued fractions and
irrationality measures (your central area), Painlevé /
isomonodromy theory (via the V_quad ⇝ P_III(D₆) reduction),
and Borel–Écalle resurgence (via the BoT channel).  The
Chowla–Selberg gamma quotients you and Cohen analyse in
arXiv:2510.00215 are direct neighbours of the CC-channel
constants targeted by CT v1.3 on the Δ < 0 side.  The
companion paper PCF-1 v1.3 (10.5281/zenodo.19937196), which
catalogues 30 degree-two PCFs and identifies a six-element
CM-field stratum on the Δ < 0 side, would also be of interest.
