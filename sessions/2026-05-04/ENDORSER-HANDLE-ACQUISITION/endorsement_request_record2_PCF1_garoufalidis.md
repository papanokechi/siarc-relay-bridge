**Subject:** arXiv endorsement request for `math.NT` submission "Complex Multiplication as a Transcendence Predicate for Degree-Two Polynomial Continued Fractions"

---

Dear Professor Garoufalidis,

I am writing to request your endorsement for an arXiv submission
to the `math.NT` category, with cross-listing to `math.CA`.

**Paper summary.** We present a transcendence predicate for
degree-two polynomial continued fractions (PCFs), based on the
sign of the balanced discriminant Δ = β² − 4αγ of the
denominator polynomial b_n = α n² + β n + γ.  Working inside
the Spec(K) classification framework, we describe a v5 upgrade
of the schema that adds the Stokes exponent and the connection-
coefficient proxy as components 6 and 7.  Our central
empirical contribution is a sharp dichotomy across thirty
degree-two families: twenty-four families in the F(2,4) Trans-
stratum have Δ ∈ {+1, +8} and admit elementary closed forms,
while six candidates with Δ ∈ {−3, −4, −7, −11, −20, −28}
all return PSLQ non-detection against an eighteen-element
constants basis at working precision 220 digits and integer
bound 10¹⁰.  We record these as Conjecture A v5
(parts (i)–(iv)).  The Painlevé III(D₆) transcendental
nature of the Δ = −11 prototype V_quad is recalled, and a
Stokes-exponent diagnostic is introduced and verified across
all six Δ < 0 degree-two families.

**Mathematical pedigree and relevance to your work.**  The
Stokes-exponent diagnostic that separates the six Δ < 0 PCFs
is built on Écalle resurgence and Berry–Howls hyperasymptotics
applied to the Wallis recurrence — the same circle of ideas
that drives Costin–Garoufalidis on resurgence of the
Kontsevich–Zagier power series and fractional polylogarithms,
and that more recently surfaces in the Habiro ring of a
number field (Garoufalidis–Scholze–Wheeler–Zagier 2024,
arXiv:2412.04241).  PCF-1 v1.3's contribution is to apply
that resurgent toolkit to the Ramanujan-Machine catalogue of
degree-two PCFs and identify a CM-field signature on the
non-elementary side.
