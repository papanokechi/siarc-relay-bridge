## Phase C.1 — synthesis paragraph (~180 words)

The L-equation, in the formal-asymptotic-theory tradition
of Birkhoff (1930) and Birkhoff–Trjitzinsky (1933) and the
Borel-summability tradition of Costin (2008, ch. 5), is the
linear differential-or-difference operator `L(y) = 0` whose
formal-series solutions `e^{Q(x)} · s(x)` are the OGF /
wave-function objects of the underlying analytic problem;
it is satisfied by — not equal to — the OGF in the
(z, w)-function-space layer. The isomonodromic deformation
of L, in the Hamiltonian-formulation tradition of Okamoto
(1987) and the Lax-pair / Riemann–Hilbert tradition of
Barhoumi–Lisovyy–Miller–Prokhorov (2024 §4.1, after Jimbo–
Miwa 1981 II), describes how the monodromy data (Stokes
constants, connection matrices) of L deform as a parameter
t varies; it lives in the (q, p, t) Hamiltonian / monodromy-
data layer, with `(q, p)` reading off solutions of P_III(D_6)
through the over-determined Lax pair `(Λ(λ, x), X(λ, x))` whose
compatibility condition `[∂_x − X, ∂_λ − Λ] = 0` returns the
deformation equation. The map Φ in CT v1.3 §3.5 acts on the
deformation-layer monodromy data — not on the L-equation
layer functions f and their derivatives directly.

## Phase C.2 — implicit-vs-explicit analysis of CT v1.3 §3.5

The CT v1.3 §3.5 wording "algebraic identity at Painlevé-
class level only" implicitly captures the layer separation —
"Painlevé-class level" is shorthand for the deformation
layer in the (q, p, t) variables, and "algebraic identity"
is shorthand for an identity reading off monodromy-data
correspondences rather than identifying the V_quad scalar
OGF with a Hamiltonian (q, p). However, the wording does
**not** spell out (i) which side of the identity is the
L-equation, (ii) which side is the deformation, (iii) that
the bridge between them is the Lax pair `(Λ, X)`, or (iv)
that the compatibility condition `[∂_x − X, ∂_λ − Λ] = 0`
is the precise reduction of the deformation back to a single
nonlinear ODE in t. A reader uncalibrated to the Painlevé
literature could plausibly read CT v1.3 §3.5 as asserting an
(incorrect) algebraic identification of the V_quad scalar
ODE with the P_III(D_6) Hamiltonian directly. Explicit
spelling reduces this risk and frames G17 as a
documentation upgrade rather than a structural gap.
