## A.1.1 Verbatim quote (slot 07 §2.1, p. 318)

Transcribed from `pdftotext -layout` extraction (lines 1215–1300):

> Let e_1, e_2 be the canonical basis of the two dimensional complex
> vector space V; we write a vector of V as v = v_1 e_1 + v_2 e_2 = (v_1, v_2).
> Recall v = (v_1, v_2) is regarded as parameters of the Painlevé system
> by means of θ_0 = v_1, θ_∞ = v_2.
>
> Consider in V the vectors:
>     α_1 = e_1 − e_2,    α_2 = e_2,    α = e_1 + e_2.
>
> Let R = R_{III'} be the root system of the type B_2. Then α_i (i=1,2)
> are the fundamental roots of R and α is the highest root.
>
> Denote by s_i the reflection of V with respect to the line
> (α_i | v) = 0, and by s_0 the reflection with respect to (α | v) = −1,
> where (v | v') is the symmetric bilinear form in V such that
> (e_i | e_j) = δ_{ij}.
>
> We have:
>     s_1 : v ↦ (v_2, v_1),
>     s_2 : v ↦ (v_1, −v_2),
>     s_0 : v ↦ (−1 − v_2, −1 − v_1).
>
> Let G be the group generated by s_1, s_2 and s_0 and W the group
> generated by s_1 and s_2. Then G is isomorphic to the affine Weyl
> group W_a(R) and W is the Weyl group W(R).

## A.1.2 Derived data

### Bilinear form
   `(e_i | e_j) = δ_{ij}` (standard inner product on V = C^2).

### Root norms (relative to (·|·))
   - `(α_1, α_1) = (e_1 − e_2, e_1 − e_2) = 1 + 1 = 2`  → α_1 is **long**.
   - `(α_2, α_2) = (e_2, e_2) = 1`                       → α_2 is **short**.
   - `(α, α)     = (e_1 + e_2, e_1 + e_2) = 2`           → α is **long**
     (highest root in B_2 is long, ✓).

### Pairing
   - `(α_1, α_2) = (e_1 − e_2, e_2) = −1`
   - `(α_1, α)   = (e_1 − e_2, e_1 + e_2) = 0`
   - `(α_2, α)   = (e_2, e_1 + e_2) = 1`

### Cartan integers `a_{ij} = 2(α_i, α_j) / (α_i, α_i)`
   - `a_{11} = 2`, `a_{22} = 2`
   - `a_{12} = 2·(−1)/2 = −1`
   - `a_{21} = 2·(−1)/1 = −2`
   - With α_0 := δ − α (the affine simple root corresponding to s_0,
     where δ is the imaginary root), `(α_0, α_j) = −(α, α_j)`:
     `(α_0, α_1) = 0`, `(α_0, α_2) = −1`.
   - `(α_0, α_0) = (α, α) = 2`.
   - `a_{00} = 2`, `a_{01} = 0`, `a_{10} = 0`, `a_{02} = −1`,
     `a_{20} = 2·(−1)/1 = −2`.

### B_2^{(1)} Cartan matrix (rows indexed 0,1,2)
```
    [  2    0   -1 ]      α_0
C = [  0    2   -1 ]      α_1
    [ -2   -2    2 ]      α_2
```

### Coxeter exponents `m_{ij}` (from `a_{ij}·a_{ji} ∈ {0,1,2,3,≥4}` →
`m ∈ {2,3,4,6,∞}`)
   - `m_{12} = m_{21} = 4` (from `(−1)(−2) = 2`)
   - `m_{02} = m_{20} = 4` (from `(−1)(−2) = 2`)
   - `m_{01} = m_{10} = 2` (from `0`)

### Defining relations of W^{aff}(B_2) (= G in Okamoto's notation)
```
   s_0^2 = s_1^2 = s_2^2 = e
   (s_1 s_2)^4 = e
   (s_2 s_0)^4 = e
   (s_1 s_0)^2 = e        (i.e. s_1 and s_0 commute)
```

### Affine B_2^{(1)} Dynkin diagram
```
      s_1 ──── s_2 ⇒ s_0
```
(single bond s_1—s_2; double bond s_2 ⇒ s_0 from short to long.)
Equivalently, with the convention that arrows point from short to
long, the diagram has a Z/2 automorphism swapping s_1 ↔ s_0 (both
nodes adjacent only to s_2 with the same single/double bond
structure relative to s_2 — wait: s_1—s_2 single, s_2⇒s_0 double).
The diagram is in fact **not** symmetric under s_0 ↔ s_1; the long-
root node and the short-root extension differ. Aut(B_2^{(1)}) = trivial
in Kac's classification (Tables Aff 1, 2, 3 in Kac 1990 §4.8).

(Note: we do **not** rely on Aut(B_2^{(1)}) for the bridge below.)

## A.1.3 Cardinality
   - `|W(B_2)| = |⟨s_1, s_2⟩| = 8` (= |D_4|).
   - `|W^{aff}(B_2)| = ∞` (translation lattice is the coroot lattice
     `Q^∨(B_2) ≅ Z^2`; finite quotient is `W(B_2) = D_4`).
   - Structure: `W^{aff}(B_2) = W(B_2) ⋉ Q^∨(B_2)`.

## A.1.4 Additional Okamoto involution (slot 07 §2.3, p. 320)

Verbatim:
> The differential equation E = E_{III'} is invariant under the
> involution of V:  y : v ↦ −v.

This y is **not** a member of G = W^{aff}(B_2) generated by
{s_0, s_1, s_2}; it is a separately-introduced involution of the
parameter space that commutes with the differential equation.

Phase B will show y is realised inside the Sakai-side codomain as
the Weyl element `r_0 · r'_0`, and is therefore in the image of the
(ungenerated-by-y) homomorphism φ.
