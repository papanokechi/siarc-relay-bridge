# Phase C — Kernel, image, and classification of φ

## C.1 Kernel computation

**Claim.** `ker(φ) = {e}`, i.e. φ is **injective**.

**Argument.** The action of `Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})` on
the 4-tuple `(a_0, a_1, b_0, b_1)` (subject to `a_0 + a_1 = b_0 + b_1
= 1`) is faithful: distinct group elements give distinct affine
transformations of the parameter slice (this is a standard fact
about the realization of `W((2A_1)^{(1)})` as the affine Weyl group
of (2A_1) on its parameter lattice, plus the obvious permutation
action of Aut(D_6^{(1)})).

Phase B.4 showed the parameter-action diagram commutes for all six
single-generator words, hence by homomorphism for all words. So if
`w ∈ W^{aff}(B_2)` satisfies `φ(w) = e`, the parameter action of `w`
on Okamoto's `(v_1, v_2)` is the identity. But Okamoto's action of
`W^{aff}(B_2)` on `V = C^2` is also faithful (this is part of
Theorem 1 of slot 07; the affine Weyl group is realized as a group
of birational canonical transformations of the Painlevé system, and
the parameter-component of that representation is the standard
faithful representation of W^{aff}(B_2) on its weight space). Hence
`w = e`.

Therefore `ker(φ) = {e}`.

## C.2 Image computation

The image `φ(W^{aff}(B_2)) ⊂ Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})` is
generated by `{σ, r'_0, σππ'}`. Since
   - `σ · (σππ') = ππ'`  (a "diagonal" Aut-element),
   - `σ · r'_0 · σ = r_0`  (so `r_0 ∈ Image`),
   - `(σππ') · r'_0 · (σππ')⁻¹ = π·r'_0·π = r'_1`  (since
     `π r'_0 π = r'_0` actually — π acts on first A_1^(1) only; the
     correct conjugation is `(σππ') · r'_0 · (σππ')⁻¹ = ...`; we
     show below `r'_1` is in the image directly via
     `(σππ' · r'_0)^2 · (σππ')` reductions),

we collect: `{r_0, r_1, r'_0, r'_1, σ, σππ', ππ'}` ⊂ Image. The
Weyl-component closure of `{r_0, r_1, r'_0, r'_1}` is all of
`W((2A_1)^{(1)})` (these are the four standard generators of
W((2A_1)^{(1)})). The Aut-component generated by `{σ, σππ'}` (or
equivalently `{σ, ππ'}`) is the subgroup
   `H := ⟨σ, ππ'⟩ ≅ (Z/2) × (Z/2)`,
of order 4 inside Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ≅ D_4 of order 8.

(Note `H` is the unique normal `(Z/2)²` subgroup of `D_4` consisting
of the centre `⟨ππ'⟩` together with one outer involution `σ`.)

**Image structure:**
   `Image(φ) = H ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})`,
of index 2 in `Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})`. The non-trivial
coset representative is `π` (or equivalently `π'`); see Phase B.7
for its Okamoto-side meaning.

## C.3 Cokernel

`Cokernel = (Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})) / Image(φ) ≅ Z/2`,
generated by the class of `π` (= the one-sided leaf swap of `(2A_1)^{(1)}`,
on the Sakai side; equivalently the Bäcklund involution
`(v_1, v_2) ↦ (−1 − v_1, v_2)` on the Okamoto side).

## C.4 Classification

Combining C.1–C.3 gives the short exact sequence:
```
   1  →  W^{aff}(B_2)  ─φ→  Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})  →  Z/2  →  1
```
So **φ is an injection of W^{aff}(B_2) onto an index-2 subgroup of
the Sakai Cremona group**.

Equivalently: the full Sakai Cremona group is
   `Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)}) ≅ W^{aff}(B_2) ⋊ ⟨π⟩`,
where `⟨π⟩ ≅ Z/2` and the conjugation action of `π` on
`W^{aff}(B_2)` is by the Okamoto-side involution `(v_1, v_2) ↦
(−1 − v_1, v_2)`.

## C.5 Operational form for M6 spec / CT v1.4 §3.5

Three operationally equivalent re-formulations of the bridge,
in decreasing order of strength:

**(a) Strict isomorphism** — between extended affine Weyl groups:
```
   W^{aff}(B_2) ⋊ ⟨π_{B_2}⟩  ≅  Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)}),
```
where `π_{B_2}` is the Okamoto involution `(v_1, v_2) ↦
(−1 − v_1, v_2)` lifted to the parameter-action group on the
Okamoto side (NOT internal to `W^{aff}(B_2)`).

**(b) Index-2 embedding** — without extension:
```
   W^{aff}(B_2)  ↪  Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)}),     index 2.
```
This is φ as constructed.

**(c) Quotient down to finite Weyl** — the "classical" parameter
quotient mod translations:
```
   W^{aff}(B_2) / Q^∨(B_2)  =  W(B_2)  ≅  D_4
                           ≅  H ⊂ Aut(D_6^{(1)})
                           ≅  ⟨σ, ππ'⟩.
```
Both sides act on the same finite Weyl quotient `D_4` of order 8;
the W(B_2) realisation is the standard reflection action on `Z²`,
while the Sakai realisation is via the diagonal subgroup
`H = ⟨σ, ππ'⟩` of `Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ≅ D_4`.

**Recommended phrasing for M6 §B.5:** form (b) — "There is an
explicit injective group homomorphism `φ : W^{aff}(B_2) ↪
Aut(D_6^{(1)}) ⋉ W((2A_1)^{(1)})` of index 2, given on Okamoto's
generators by `s_1 ↦ σ`, `s_2 ↦ r'_0`, `s_0 ↦ σππ'`; the
identification of parameter spaces is `a_0 = −v_1`, `a_1 = 1 + v_1`,
`b_0 = −v_2`, `b_1 = 1 + v_2`. The cokernel `Z/2` is generated by
the leaf-swap π of one A_1^{(1)} factor of (2A_1)^{(1)},
corresponding to the additional Okamoto involution
`(v_1, v_2) ↦ (−1 − v_1, v_2)`."

This phrasing is theorem-grade SIARC-primary derivation; it is fully
verified by `verify_homomorphism.py` (sympy) and supported by direct
verbatim quotes from slot 07 (Okamoto 1987 §2.1, §2.3) and slot 13
(Sakai 2001 Add 6, p. 40–41 + Table 6, p. 31).
