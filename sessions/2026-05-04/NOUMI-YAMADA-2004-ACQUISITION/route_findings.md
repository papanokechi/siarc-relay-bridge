**Critical Phase A finding: there is NO dedicated PIII / PIII' chapter.**

The book is structured around a unified affine-Weyl-group + τ-function
framework. The AMS abstract explicitly states the worked examples are
"especially those of types II and IV" (corresponding to
W(\tilde A_1) and W(\tilde A_2) — i.e., simply-laced A-type root
systems). PIII (W(B_2) in Okamoto's framing, W((2A_1)^(1)) in Sakai's)
is NOT a featured example.

Free preview: AMS exposes only the front/back matter + chapter PDF
links behind eBook authentication. The TOC alone is sufficient to
conclude the book is not the right anchor for the W(B_2) ↔
W((2A_1)^(1)) homomorphism.

Disposition: PAYWALL_PARTIAL_TOC_ONLY (Rule 1: no auth-fetch).

The spec §1 Tier-2 list mentioned `math/9806032` for "Symmetries in
the fourth Painlevé equation and Okamoto polynomials". This is
incorrect: math/9806032 is Schlichenmaier's "Sugawara Construction
for Higher Genus Riemann Surfaces" (Rept.Math.Phys.43:323-339,1999).

The correct arXiv ID for Noumi-Yamada "Symmetries in PIV and Okamoto
polynomials" (Nagoya Math. J. 153 (1999) 53-86) is **q-alg/9708018**
(submitted 1997-08-19, 25pp). This paper is PIV-focused (W(\tilde A_2)
symmetry) and does not contain a W(B_2) ↔ W((2A_1)^(1)) bridge for
PIII; therefore it was NOT downloaded as it falls outside the
acquisition target.

## Best-OA-route decision

The 3 acquired arXiv preprints are the best OA evidence available
without operator-side ILL or institutional auth. They cover:
- KNY 2017 (slot 14): the **post-2004** comprehensive review by all
  three principal authors; if the W(B_2) ↔ W((2A_1)^(1)) bridge
  existed in NY-circle thinking by 2017, it would be referenced here.
- NY 1998 (slot 15): the foundational paper that NY 2004 elaborates.
- NY 2000 (slot 16): the abstract Weyl-action framework that NY 2004
  applies.

Phase D readback verdict (see homomorphism_pin.md): the explicit
bridge does NOT appear in any of these three sources, which strongly
suggests it would also be ABSENT from NY 2004 (since the 2004 book is
a synthesis of the same line of thought, with the additional
constraint that its TOC has no PIII chapter at all).
