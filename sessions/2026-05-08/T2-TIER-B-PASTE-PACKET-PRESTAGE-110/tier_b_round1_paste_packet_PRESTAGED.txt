--- Okamoto 1987 §1 (slot 07 .txt lines 136-230) ---

Introduction.
The present article concerns the third Painlevé equation:

P_III:  d²q/dt² = (1/q)(dq/dt)² − (1/t)(dq/dt)
                + (1/t)(α q² + β) + γ q³ + δ/q,

for which we make the assumption γ δ ≠ 0, throughout this paper.
This equation is well-known and investigated by many authors
(for example, [3], [5], [6], [7]), while we consider in the
following mainly the equation:

P_III':  d²q/dt² = (1/q)(dq/dt)² − (1/t)(dq/dt)
                 + q²/(4 t²) (γ q + α) + β/(4 t) + δ/(4 q),

instead. These two equations are equivalent each other. In fact,
by replacing in P_III, t² by t and q by t q, we obtain P_III'.
Therefore a result on P_III' can be translated immediately to
that of P_III. We do not repeat results one by one. We sum up in
1.1 known facts about the equation P_III' or P_III. As for the
origin of the equation P_III', refer to [8], [10].

The Hamiltonian associated with P_III is:

H_III = (1/t) [ 2 q² p² − {2 η_∞ t q² + (2 θ_0 + 1) q
                          − 2 η_0 t} p
              + η_∞ (θ_0 + θ_∞) t q ],

where the constants η_Δ, θ_Δ (Δ = 0, ∞) are connected to α, β,
γ, δ of the equation as follows:

(0.1)        α = -4 η_∞ θ_∞,
             β = 4 η_0 (θ_0 + 1),
             γ = 4 η_∞²,
             δ = -4 η_0².

By the assumption, we have η_Δ ≠ 0; moreover we set η_Δ = 1
without loss of generality. On the other hand, the Hamiltonian
associated with P_III' is:

H_III' = (1/t) [ q² p² − {η_∞ q² + θ_0 q − η_0 t} p
               + ½ η_∞ (θ_0 + θ_∞) q ].

These two Hamiltonians are connected mutually through the
canonical transformation φ:

(0.2)   q → t q,    p → t^{-1} p,    t → t²,
        H_III' → (1/(2t))(H_III + (1/t) q p).
