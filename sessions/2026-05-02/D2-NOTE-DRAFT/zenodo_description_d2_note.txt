Newton-polygon universality of the Borel-singularity radius for polynomial continued fractions

<!-- ===== Description ===== -->

Newton-polygon universality of the Borel-singularity radius for polynomial continued fractions: a 4-page consolidatory short note (v1.0, 2026-05-02).

Position. We isolate, as a single citable artefact, the cross-degree identity ξ₀(b) = d / β_d^(1/d) for the leading Borel-plane singular distance of the formal generating function f(z) = Σ Q_n zⁿ of a polynomial continued fraction (1, b) of degree d with leading coefficient β_d > 0. The identity is currently distributed across three SIARC artefacts: the canonical d = 2 Newton-polygon proof (Channel Theory v1.3), the d = 4 verification at dps = 80 across 8 quartic representatives with spread 0 (PCF2-SESSION-Q1, 2026-05-01), and the original d = 2 baseline (PCF-1 v1.3). This note is a NEW concept DOI (no predecessor) and does not introduce new numerical pipelines; every numerical fact is carried forward from an upstream AEAL entry on the SIARC bridge.

Triple framing (epistemic tiers). The note is structured around three explicit tiers, with strict epistemic discipline on the verb choice in each:

- PROVEN at d = 2. The Newton-polygon argument of Proposition 3.3.A of Channel Theory v1.3 / Theorem 5 §5 of PCF-1 v1.3 yields the slope-1/2 edge of multiplicity 2 in the Newton polygon of the order-2 ODE governing f(z), with characteristic polynomial 1 − (β₂/4) c² and unique positive real root c = 2/√β₂ = ξ₀.

- VERIFIED at d = 4. PCF2-SESSION-Q1 (2026-05-01) measures ξ₀(b) = 4/β₄^(1/4) at dps = 80 across 8 quartic representatives drawn from the four trichotomy bins of the session, with spread 0 in the inferred c(4); the data are consistent with the slope-1/4 edge of multiplicity 2 and characteristic polynomial 1 − (β₄/4⁴) c⁴ predicted by the same Newton-polygon construction. As a corollary the v1.1 candidate c(d) = 2√((d−1)!) is ruled out at d = 4 (predicts ≈ 4.899, measured 4 with spread 0; ≈ 22% disagreement) — Remark 3.3.E of Channel Theory v1.3.

- CONJECTURED for general d ≥ 2. Conjecture 3.3.A* of Channel Theory v1.3 records the universal form ξ₀(b) = d/β_d^(1/d) and packages the d = 2 proof and the d = 4 measurement into a single cross-degree statement. The structural Newton-polygon picture (slope-1/d edge of multiplicity 2, characteristic polynomial 1 − (β_d/d^d) c^d) is recorded as a heuristic, not a proof; the indicial-polynomial layer fixing the secondary exponent at d ≥ 3 and the Birkhoff recursion for the formal coefficients are not written out and are open even at d = 3.

- DEFERRED at d = 3. The op:xi0-d3-direct entry of Channel Theory v1.3 §9 (one Newton-polygon test per Galois bin on a cubic representative at dps = 80, predicting ξ₀ = 3/β_3^(1/3)) is the next discrete step after the d = 2 proof and the d = 4 measurement. Tractability is 1–2 hours per bin with no halt condition expected.

Out of scope. The β_d < 0 branch, the d = 2 anomaly Galois bin (PCF-1 v1.3 Remark 5.E), and the d ≥ 5 case are out of scope and recorded as open in §5 of the note.

What this note is and is not. The D2-NOTE is a consolidatory cite-target, intended to be the single citation for the cross-degree ξ₀ universality axis of the SIARC umbrella. It does NOT itself prove the general-d case. The structural sketch in §4 is recorded as a heuristic. The d = 3 verification is deferred to op:xi0-d3-direct on the operator queue.
