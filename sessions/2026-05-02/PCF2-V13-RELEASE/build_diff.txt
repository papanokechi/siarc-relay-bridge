\subsection{Finer cubic split: from $\log|\Delta_3|$ to the                     
Petersson modular discriminant                                                  
(Sessions~R1, R1.1, R1.2, R1.3, T2)}\label{sec:R1-finer-split}                  
This subsection is the principal locus of v1.3's absorption of                  
the SIARC R/T cycle. It traces the evolution of the cubic-modular               
finer-split signal across five sessions, ending in the structural               
modular-form framing established by Session~T2 (2026-05-02) and                 
the cubic-only restriction established by Session~R1.3 (2026-05-01).            
\paragraph{R1: $\log|\Delta_3|$ as candidate slow-trend invariant               
(provisional, 2026-05-01).}                                                     
The original R1 catalogue-level rank-correlation battery against                
$\delta = A_{\mathrm{fit}} - 6$ on the $50$ cubic families was run              
with twelve invariants of $b(n)$ (Galois group, $\mathrm{sgn}(\Delta_3)$,       
$|\Delta_3| \bmod 8/12$, $\log|\Delta_3|$, $\omega(|\Delta_3|)$,                
smallest prime divisor, real signature, squarefree quadratic                    
resolvent on the CM bin, parity). Six number-field invariants                   
($h$, $h^+$, conductor, regulator, fundamental-unit norm, unit                  
density) were uncomputable in the available environment and recorded            
as NaN. The strongest single-invariant signal was on $\log|\Delta_3|$:          
Spearman $\rho=-0.451$, $p=1.0\times 10^{-3}$, Bonferroni-corrected             
$p=1.1\times 10^{-2}$ ($m=11$), with the signal sharpening to                   
$\rho=-0.492$ on excluding the loose-fit family~31 and weakening                
under $A_{\mathrm{stderr}}^{-2}$ inverse-variance weighting                     
($|\rho|_{\mathrm{w}}=0.26$). The R1 HALT threshold ($|\rho|>0.6$ at            
Bonferroni $p<10^{-3}$) was not met; Conjecture~B4 was retained and             
$\log|\Delta_3|$ flagged as a candidate finer-cubic-split invariant             
pending an R1.1 follow-up cycle.                                                
\paragraph{R1.1: the $j$-invariant signal upgrade (2026-05-01).}                
Session~R1.1 (the canonical follow-up cycle to R1) replaced the                 
twelve-invariant battery with a single arithmetically motivated                 
candidate, the $j$-invariant of the genus-one curve $E_b: y^2 = b(x)$           
associated to each cubic family. The result was a decisive sharpening           
of the R1 signal: Spearman                                                      
$\rho_{\mathrm{Spearman}}(\log|j(E_b)|,\, A_{\mathrm{fit}}-6) = -0.691$,        
Bonferroni $p=4\times 10^{-7}$ across the $50$-family cubic                     
catalogue (working precision $\mathrm{dps}=80$, $n_{\max}=200$).                
Four families saturate the equianharmonic CM-locus $j=0$ exactly:               
$(\alpha_3,\alpha_2,\alpha_1,\alpha_0) = (1,-3,3,a_0)$ with                     
$a_0\in\{-3,1,2,3\}$ (catalogue families~30--33); each lies within              
$10^{-3}$ of $A_{\mathrm{fit}}=6$ at the R1.1 working precision. This           
sharper signal exceeds the R1 HALT threshold and motivated the v1.2             
restatement of \texttt{op:finer-cubic-split} as ``the $\log|j|$                 
correlation pending degree-extension cross-check''.                             
\paragraph{R1.2: $d=4$ deep-WKB null (2026-05-01).}                             
Session~R1.2 probed the natural extension of the R1.1 signal to the             
quartic catalogue of Session~Q1. At $d=4$ the equianharmonic                    
$j(\mathrm{Jac}(C_b))=0$ cell is sparse; the Q1 catalogue contains              
exactly one $j=0$ representative (family~32,                                    
$b(n)=n^4-3n^3-3n^2+3n-3$). At $\mathrm{dps}=2000$, fit window                  
$N\in[50,250]$, $N_{\mathrm{ref}}=400$, the family~32 residual is               
$\delta_4 = -3.71\times 10^{-3}$ (about $48\sigma$ from $0$), and               
the Spearman correlation                                                        
$\rho_{\mathrm{Spearman}}(\log|j|,\,\delta_4) = +0.073$                         
($\mathrm{raw}\,p = 0.58$) is statistically null and of opposite                
sign from the $d=3$ signal. R1.2 therefore halted on a clean                    
two-tier outcome: B5/B6 (in any verbatim degree-agnostic form) do               
not extend to $d=4$.                                                            
\paragraph{R1.3: cleanup, CASE~B with C-caveat (2026-05-01).}                   
Session~R1.3 ran the deep-WKB cleanup ($\mathrm{dps}=5000$,                     
$N_{\mathrm{ref}}=1000$, fit window $N\in[200,800]$) on the unique              
$d=4$ $j=0$ representative (family~32) against a non-$j=0$ baseline             
of matched coefficient norm (family~1, $b(n)=n^4-3n^3-3n^2-3n-3$).              
The result is statistical indistinguishability:                                 
$\delta^{\mathrm{deep}}_4(\mathrm{fam}~32) = -4.55\times 10^{-4}$               
versus                                                                          
$\delta^{\mathrm{deep}}_4(\mathrm{fam}~01) = -4.60\times 10^{-4}$,              
difference $+4.9\times 10^{-6}$ (0.24 pooled standard errors). The              
sharp form of B5 ($\delta_4\to 0$ for $j=0$ quartics, sharper than              
for non-$j=0$ ones) is rejected at $d=4$. The $d=3$ Conjecture~B4               
fixed-$A=6$ residualization is reproduced at $\rho=-0.5683$,                    
Bonferroni $p=5.0\times 10^{-5}$, $\mathrm{dps}=2000$,                          
$N\in[50,250]$, $N_{\mathrm{ref}}=400$. The shallow-$N$ extension               
to $99$ new $j=0$ quartics shows a real but sub-leading shift                   
(median $\delta^{\mathrm{free}}_4 = -2.49\times 10^{-3}$ vs.\                   
non-$j=0$ Q1 median $-3.73\times 10^{-3}$, Mann--Whitney                        
$U=3893$, two-sided $p=4.8\times 10^{-4}$; survives stratification              
on coefficient sum, $p=1.1\times 10^{-6}$ in the band                           
$[6,12]$), but R1.3-D shows the apparent shift dissolves with                   
$N_{\max}$ in lockstep with the non-$j=0$ baseline. The verdict is              
\textsc{CASE~B with C-caveat}: B5/B6 are confirmed $d=3$-specific               
in their sharp/deep form; the shallow-$N$ effect at $d=4$ is real but           
sub-leading and not extensible to a deep-$N$ rule.

\paragraph{AEAL schema (v1.3 canonical form).}\label{para:aeal-schema}          
For unambiguous machine-checkable cross-referencing across the SIARC            
bridge, every numerical claim attached to a session is logged as a              
single line of \texttt{claims.jsonl} in the JSON schema                         
\begin{verbatim}                                                                
{"claim": "...",                                                                
 "evidence_type": "computation",                                                
 "dps": <int>,                                                                  
 "reproducible": <bool>,                                                        
 "script": "<filename>",                                                        
 "output_hash": "<sha256>"}                                                     
\end{verbatim}                                                                  
This is the schema in actual use across all sessions cited in the               
present document; it is canonical from PCF-2~v1.3 forward and                   
supersedes any earlier informal references. We note three operational           
clarifications: (a)~a \texttt{claim\_id} field is added by the                  
aggregator on demand when cross-referencing claims between sessions,            
and is not part of the per-session record; (b)~a \texttt{verdict}               
field, when present, takes values in                                            
$\{\text{\texttt{PROVED}},\,\text{\texttt{FALSIFIED}},\,                        
   \text{\texttt{PERFORMED}},\,\text{\texttt{PARTIAL}}\}$;                      
(c)~the \texttt{output\_hash} is the SHA-256 of the script's stdout             
or of the JSON output the script writes, whichever is the canonical             
artefact for the claim. This paragraph addresses Fleet-F audit                  
finding F1-01 (AEAL schema drift), and the v1.3 release-level                   
claims.jsonl in                                                                 
\texttt{sessions/2026-05-02/PCF2-V13-RELEASE/claims.jsonl} is the               
first PCF-series record that is schema-compliant by design.

the natural follow-up. Multi-invariant rank regression yields
