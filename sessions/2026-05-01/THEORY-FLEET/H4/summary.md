## 1. Resurgence framework relevant to a Borel branch point

Let the formal Birkhoff expansion be written, after the project convention is fixed, as
\[
\Phi(x) \sim \sum_{n\ge 0} a_n x^{n+\lambda},
\]
with Gevrey-1 growth. Its Borel transform is schematically
\[
\widehat\Phi(\zeta)=\sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{a_n}{\Gamma(n+\lambda+1)}\zeta^{n+\lambda}.
\]
A resurgent function is one whose Borel transform analytically continues with controlled singularities. Écalle's three Orsay volumes introduce the algebra of such functions, alien derivations, and the bridge-equation/median machinery [@Ecalle1981ResurgentesI; @Ecalle1981ResurgentesII; @Ecalle1985ResurgentesIII]. Sauzin's notes give a concrete introduction to alien calculus in nonlinear difference-equation and splitting problems, emphasizing that alien derivatives measure the singular behavior at Borel singularities and encode exponentially small Stokes jumps [@Sauzin2007Splitting, Secs. 4--5]. Mitschi--Sauzin, LNM 2153, gives a modern book-length treatment of 1-summability, resurgence, Borel-plane singularities, and alien calculus [@MitschiSauzin2016LNM2153, Ch. 6].

For a ray direction `θ`, the Stokes automorphism is generated by the alien derivatives located on that ray. In the common one-scale notation,
\[
\mathfrak S_\theta
 = \exp\!\left(\sum_{\arg \omega=\theta} e^{-\omega/x}\Delta_\omega\right),
\]
where `Δ_ω` extracts the singular germ at `ω` [@Dorigoni2019Intro, Sec. 5; @AnicetoSchiappa2015Transseries, Sec. 2]. If `ζ*` is the first singularity on the positive ray, the lateral Borel sums across that ray obey
\[
\mathcal S_{0^+}\Phi-\mathcal S_{0^-}\Phi
  = \mathcal S_{0^-}\big((\mathfrak S_0-1)\Phi\big)
  \approx e^{-\zeta_*/x} S_{\zeta_*}\,\mathcal S\Phi_{\zeta_*}+
  \text{higher instantons}.
\]
Here `S_{ζ*}` is the Stokes coefficient and `Φ_{ζ*}` is the formal sector reached by `Δ_{ζ*}`. The median sum removes the ambiguity by applying the half-Stokes automorphism,
\[
\mathcal S_{\rm med}=
\mathcal S_{0^-}\circ\mathfrak S_0^{1/2}
 = \mathcal S_{0^+}\circ\mathfrak S_0^{-1/2},
\]
which is the standard real/unambiguous prescription for real transseries on a Stokes line [@Dorigoni2019Intro, Sec. 6.4; @AnicetoSchiappa2015Transseries].

The local singularity need not be a simple pole. In simple resurgence it often has logarithmic alien structure, while the coefficient-level effect may look algebraic if the target sector has a fractional prefactor:
\[
\widehat\Phi(\zeta)
\sim {S_{\zeta_*}\over 2\pi i}\,
(\zeta-\zeta_*)^{-\beta}
\left(c_0+c_1(\zeta-\zeta_*)+\cdots\right)
+\text{regular/log companion}.
\]
The empirical information says **branch point, not pole**. The square-root lift result suggests a square-root-class algebraic component, but the exact exponent should be measured from large-order data rather than assumed. Thus the safest classification is: **simple-resurgent algebraic/logarithmic branch point, expected square-root class**.

## 2. Recipe for extracting the Stokes coefficient at `ζ*`

The extraction should avoid global Padé-Laplace evaluation as the primary estimator. Padé is useful for visualizing the branch cut, but the coefficient `S_{ζ*}` is encoded directly in the **large-order tail** of the original coefficients by Borel-Darboux/resurgence relations [@Costin2008Borel; @AnicetoSchiappaVonk2012Instantons; @Dorigoni2019Intro].

### Step A — fix normalization

Record the exact series convention used for `V_quad`: expansion variable, factorial normalization, sign convention, and whether the Borel transform uses `Γ(n+1)`, `Γ(n+λ+1)`, or a shifted convention. This is essential because the reported Stokes coefficient changes by elementary powers of `ζ*`, `2πi`, and sector normalizations.

### Step B — lock the singular action

Use the analytically established value
\[
A=\zeta_*=4/\sqrt{3}
\]
rather than refitting it. The action should be treated as exact to the working precision. This removes one of the main error sources in large-order analysis.

### Step C — infer the branch exponent

From a coefficient ansatz
\[
a_n \sim C\,A^{-n-\beta}\Gamma(n+\beta)
       \left(1+{\mu_1\over n+\beta-1}+{\mu_2\over (n+\beta-1)(n+\beta-2)}+\cdots\right),
\]
fit `β` using ratio and difference estimators such as
\[
A{a_{n+1}\over a_n}-n = \beta + O(1/n).
\]
If the branch is square-root-class, `β` should be a half-integer after the correct normalization. If a logarithmic companion is present, the estimator may show `log n/n` drift; then include logarithmic corrections in the asymptotic model.

### Step D — extract the Stokes amplitude by alien/large-order matching

After `β` is fixed, form
\[
R_n={a_n A^{n+\beta}\over \Gamma(n+\beta)}.
\]
Fit
\[
R_n \sim C\left(1+{r_1\over n}+{r_2\over n(n-1)}+\cdots\right)
\]
using only high-order windows, then accelerate the `n\to\infty` limit with Richardson/Weniger-type transforms. The Stokes coefficient is obtained from
\[
S_{\zeta_*}=2\pi i\,C/c_0^{(\zeta_*)},
\]
where `c_0^{(ζ*)}` is the chosen normalization of the adjacent formal sector. If that sector is normalized to leading coefficient 1, then `S_{ζ*}=2πi C`; otherwise the sector normalization must be divided out.

### Step E — local Borel singularity cross-check

Use Padé or conformal analytic continuation only locally: continue the Borel transform to a punctured neighborhood of `ζ*`, subtract fitted analytic regular terms, and fit the singular germ. This determines whether the local model is pure algebraic, logarithmic, or algebraic-logarithmic. The local fit and the coefficient-tail fit must agree for `S_{ζ*}`.

### Step F — median-resummation sign check

Compute the predicted discontinuity symbolically/numerically from the extracted alien derivative:
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{0}\Phi \equiv
\mathcal S_{0^+}\Phi-\mathcal S_{0^-}\Phi
\approx e^{-A/x}S_{A}\Phi_A.
\]
The median prescription inserts half of this jump with the sign fixed by the selected lateral convention. This check is necessary because large-order data often determines `|S_A|` more robustly than the sign/phase under differing Borel-normalization conventions.

### Step G — closed-form recognition only after stability

Only after 30+ stable digits are obtained should PSLQ be attempted. Candidate bases should be ordered by geometry: first `π`, powers of `2` and `3`, and `Γ(1/3), Γ(2/3), Γ(1/6), Γ(5/6)`; then PIII(D6)-monodromy elementary trigonometric/gamma constants; only later `Γ(1/4)`/Catalan and zeta-values. The exact `4/√3` strongly suggests `Q(√3)`/equianharmonic geometry rather than square-lattice Catalan geometry.

## 3. Precision forecast

The Padé wall at about seven digits is expected for global approximation to a function with branch cuts. It is not an intrinsic information bound. In contrast, large-order extraction uses the last thousands of coefficients and the exact singular action. With 5000 coefficients at 250 dps:

- **Robust target:** 30 stable digits.
- **Likely target:** 35--45 stable digits.
- **Optimistic target:** 50 digits if the next singularity is sufficiently separated and the branch exponent is a simple rational.

The dominant risks are: an unrecognized nearby singularity with similar modulus, wrong factorial shift, mixed conjugate actions, or a logarithmic companion not included in the fit. If all these are controlled, 30+ digits should be routine.

## 4. Closed-form prospects

The literature supports closed forms for Stokes data in special integrable/isomonodromic problems, but it does not imply that every Stokes constant is elementary. The strongest candidates for `V_quad` are:

1. **Gamma products at thirds/sixths**: `Γ(1/3)`, `Γ(2/3)`, `Γ(1/6)`, `Γ(5/6)`, with powers of `π`, `2`, and `3`. Rationale: `ζ*=4/√3` and related `Q(√{-3})`/equianharmonic geometry.
2. **Elementary PIII(D6) monodromy constants**: sines/cosines/gamma ratios depending on monodromy exponents, if the `V_quad` normalization maps directly to a known PIII(D6) Riemann-Hilbert normalization.
3. **Zeta-values**: more likely in fluctuation coefficients around the singularity than in the leading Stokes multiplier.
4. **Catalan/Γ(1/4)**: lower priority unless an independent square-lattice/`τ=i` period appears.

Thus: closed form is **testable but not established**.
