Dear Editor,

I am submitting the enclosed manuscript, "Complex Multiplication
as a Transcendence Predicate for Degree-Two Polynomial Continued
Fractions," for consideration as a research article in
Compositio Mathematica.

Headline contribution.  The paper introduces a Stokes-exponent
diagnostic S(t) for one-parameter deformations of polynomial
continued fractions (PCFs) and verifies a clean experimental
signature (S < 1) on every known degree-two PCF whose denominator
discriminant is negative, across all six available CM
discriminants Delta in {-3,-4,-7,-11,-20,-28}.  An intra-field
replication using two distinct PCFs in Q(sqrt(-7)) -- one Heegner
(class number 1, Delta = -7) and one non-Heegner (class number 2,
Delta = -28) -- shows that the structural carrier of the signal
is the CM field itself, not the class number.  This is, to our
knowledge, the first cross-family experimental evidence
identifying complex multiplication as a transcendence predicate
at the level of two-coefficient integer recurrences.

Mathematical content.  The paper combines (i) a proven theorem
for the Delta > 0 side (closed-form structure of the F(2,4)
Trans-stratum, Theorem 3.1), (ii) an extensive empirical
dichotomy across thirty degree-two PCF families
(Table 1: 22 + 2 + 6), (iii) the new Stokes-exponent diagnostic
and its six-family verification (Section 5), (iv) the
intra-field replication ruling out the Heegner-number hypothesis
(Section 5.5), and (v) a recall of the V_quad Painleve III(D6)
prototype with explicit Stokes constant 0.43770528...
(Section 6).  Section 7 documents a fully reproducible
high-precision computational pipeline.  Six concrete open
problems are stated.  The conjectural status of the Delta < 0
side is maintained throughout: the experimental evidence is
clearly distinguished from the proved Delta > 0 part.

Honesty about experimental content.  The principal new
mathematical assertions are conjectures (Conjecture A v5,
parts (i), (iii)).  Their support is high-precision numerical:
PSLQ non-detection across an 18-element constants basis at
working precision 220 digits and integer bound 1e10, plus
Stokes-exponent measurements at dps = 250 and depth = 1500.
We make no transcendence claim that goes beyond what the
Schneider/Lang/Bertrand/Wolfart/Wustholz lineage establishes
at the level of CM elliptic periods; the novelty is the
descent of the same dichotomy to the elementary level of
Z-coefficient continued fractions, which we believe is of
independent interest to readers in transcendence theory,
isomonodromy, and experimental mathematics.

Why Compositio.  The paper sits at the interface of
transcendence theory, isomonodromic deformation theory of the
Heun-Painleve correspondence, and experimental number theory.
Compositio Mathematica regularly publishes papers in this
character range (e.g., Bertrand-Beukers periods of CM curves,

across CM, dynamics on homogeneous spaces, and arithmetic geometry

dichotomy theorem on the Delta > 0 side with a sharp empirical
conjecture and Stokes-exponent diagnostic on the Delta < 0 side.
