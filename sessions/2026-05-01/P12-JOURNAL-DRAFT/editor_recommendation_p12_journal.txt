Editor recommendation paragraph (one paragraph for the
"recommended editor" field of the Trans. AMS submission portal):

We respectfully suggest assignment to an associate editor with
expertise in transcendence theory or in isomonodromy /
Painleve theory.  The mathematical core of the paper is a
sharp dichotomy in the closed-form behaviour of degree-two
polynomial continued fractions, controlled by the sign of a
balanced discriminant Delta = beta^2 - 4 alpha gamma.  When
Delta > 0 (Theorem 3.1) every value admits an elementary
closed form in the field generated by pi, e, log Q^x,
zeta(3), and quadratic surds; when Delta < 0 (Conjecture A
v5) PSLQ returns no relation against an 18-element basis,
and a new Stokes-exponent diagnostic confirms a fractional-
power singularity under CM-respecting deformations on every
one of the six known Delta < 0 families, including a
non-Heegner intra-field cross-check that rules out the
class-number hypothesis.  Editors familiar with the
Schneider / Lang / Bertrand / Wolfart / Wustholz lineage
of CM transcendence results, with the Heun-Painleve
isomonodromy literature in the spirit of Okamoto and
Iwasaki-Kimura-Shimomura-Yoshida, with Borel-Ecalle
resurgence and Stokes-constant computation, or with PSLQ
integer-relation methods of Ferguson and Bailey, would all
be in strong position to evaluate the manuscript.  Editors
with experience in experimental and computer-assisted
mathematics, including recent Ramanujan-Machine-style
programmes, would also be appropriate given that the
empirical evidence underlies the central conjectural claim.
