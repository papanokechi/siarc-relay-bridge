\subsection{Finer-cubic-split: the $j$-invariant of $E_b$}
\label{sec:finer-split-j}

The 50 cubic catalogue families of Sections~\ref{sec:catalogue}
(Sessions B/C1) all satisfy $A_{\mathrm{fit}} = 2d = 6$ to within
the WKB extraction error $A_{\mathrm{stderr}} \le 0.08$ at
$\mathrm{dps} = 800$ (Conjecture~B4 sharp form). A finer-split
probe (PCF2-SESSION-R1.1, $\mathrm{dps} = 1500$, 61 fit points,
$N \in [10, 130]$) reveals a closed-form sub-rule controlled by the
$j$-invariant of the elliptic curve
\[
   E_b \;:\; y^2 \;=\; b(x), \qquad
   j(E_b) \;=\; \frac{c_4(E_b)^3}{\Delta(E_b)}.
\]
Let $\delta(b) := A_{\mathrm{PCF}}(b) - 6$ and
$\ell_j(b) := \log\bigl(\,|j(E_b)| + 1\,\bigr)$.
Across the 50-family catalogue, the Spearman rank correlation between
$\delta(b)$ and $\ell_j(b)$ is
\[
   \rho_{\mathrm{S}}(\ell_j, \delta) \;=\; -0.691,
   \qquad p_{\mathrm{raw}} \;=\; 2.9 \cdot 10^{-8},
   \qquad p_{\mathrm{Bonf}} \;=\; 4.0 \cdot 10^{-7}
\]
($K = 14$ effective tests in a 22-invariant battery; details
in~\cite{R11}). The signal is independent of $\log|\Delta_3(b)|$
($\rho_{\mathrm{S}}(\ell_j, \log|\Delta_3|) = -0.016$, $p = 0.91$);
the partial Spearman $\rho(\ell_j, \delta \mid \log|\Delta_3|) = -0.794$
($p = 5.9 \cdot 10^{-12}$) confirms $\ell_j$ as the primary
finer-split coordinate and $\log|\Delta_3|$ as a secondary.

\begin{conjecture}[B5 — $j$-vanishing locus]
\label{conj:b5}
For every $\mathbb{Z}$-primitive irreducible cubic
$b(x) = a_3 x^3 + a_2 x^2 + a_1 x + a_0$ in the PCF-2 scope,
\[
   j(E_b) = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; A_{\mathrm{PCF}}(b) \;=\; 2d \;=\; 6
   \quad \text{exactly}.
\]
Empirically: the four families $b_{1,-3,3,c}(n) = n^3 - 3n^2 + 3n + c$,
$c \in \{-3, 1, 2, 3\}$, have $j(E_b) = 0$ (the equianharmonic CM-locus
$\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$); their $A_{\mathrm{fit}}$ values are
$\{5.998856, 5.999882, 6.000489, 6.001004\}$ with
$A_{\mathrm{stderr}} \le 5.4 \cdot 10^{-4}$, agreement with $2d = 6$
to $\le 1.0 \cdot 10^{-3}$ on every family.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[B6 — soft drift]
\label{conj:b6}
For $j(E_b) \neq 0$, there is a non-decreasing function
$\varepsilon : \mathbb{R}_{> 0} \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ with
$\varepsilon(0^+) = 0$ such that
\[
   A_{\mathrm{PCF}}(b) \;=\; 2d \;-\; \varepsilon\bigl(\,|j(E_b)|\,\bigr)
   \;+\; o(1)
\]
as the catalogue is exhausted in lexicographic order. Empirically
$\varepsilon$ is monotone in $\log|j|$ on the cubic catalogue with
slope $\approx 4.5 \cdot 10^{-3}$ per unit of $\log|j|$
(linear-rank-regression coefficient).
\end{conjecture}

\begin{remark}[Mahler-measure secondary signal]
The Mahler measure $M(b)$ is also rank-correlated with $\delta(b)$
($\rho_{\mathrm{S}} = -0.371$, raw $p = 8.0 \cdot 10^{-3}$,
Bonferroni $p \approx 0.11$ at $K = 14$). The bivariate
$(\log M(b), \log|j(E_b)|)$ rank-regression reaches adjusted
$R^2 = 0.54$. We do not promote $M(b)$ to a separate conjecture
because of partial collinearity with $\ell_j$ (curves with $j = 0$
also tend to have small Mahler measure on this catalogue).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Open: degree-$d$ generalisation]
Conjecture~B5 is currently empirical at $d = 3$ on the lex window
$a_3 \in \{1,2,3,5,7\}$, $a_2, a_1, a_0 \in \{-3,\ldots,3\}$. The
analogous $j$-vanishing condition at $d = 4$ would be the curve
$y^2 = b(x)$ having a $\mathbb{Q}$-rational hyperelliptic involution
fixing the equianharmonic locus on its Jacobian. Verifying B5 at
$d \ge 4$ (op:b4-degree-d, op:j-vanishing-d) is the natural
follow-up.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Number-field invariants deferred]
The R1.1 probe was unable to compute the six pari/gp-derived
field-level invariants (class number $h$, narrow class number
$h^+$, conductor, regulator, fundamental-unit norm, unit density)
because pari/gp was not available in the relay environment. None
of the 14 effective tests were field-level. Conjecture~B5 may admit
a still-finer split on $h$, conductor, or regulator
(op:cubic-fielddata-r12); the absence of such tests in this draft
is the principal open question.
\end{remark}
