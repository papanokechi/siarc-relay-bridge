\paragraph{Status (CC-PIPELINE-F, 2026-05-01).}
A first attempt to numerically extract the CC-channel datum
$(\xi_{0}, \beta_{\exp}, S_{1})$ for $\Vquad$ from the
BoT-channel trans-series $\{h_{k}\}$ of $\log|\dn|$ via
Domb--Sykes ratio analysis was carried out under the
scope-limited brief of CC-PIPELINE-F. The recovery
\emph{failed}: the literature anchor $\xi_{0} = 2/\sqrt{3}$
was matched to $-0.5$ digits across two precision tiers
(depth $\in \{240, 360\}$, $\mathrm{dps} \in \{4000, 6000\}$,
$K \in \{22, 32\}$), well below the $20$-digit prompt
threshold. The \emph{structural} reading is that the BoT
trans-series $1/n$-expansion of $\log|\dn|$ is a different
formal series from the Gevrey-$1$ formal solution at $z=0$
of the linear ODE attached to $f(z) = \sum Q_{n} z^{n}$ (the
Birkhoff-factored asymptotic series at $n=\infty$ of the
underlying difference operator), which is where the
literature $\xi_{0}$ lives. This sharpens
\Cref{def:channel}: $\BoT$ and $\CC$ differ in their
formal-series space $D$, not just in their section $S$. The
re-test of Q$_{L15}$ and Q$_{L26}$ in the same pipeline
returned no $P_{III}(D_{6})$ ratio fingerprint
($\beta/\xi_{0}=-1/6$) at $\geq 15$ digits, so the
\cite{siarc_borel_kscan} ``BOTH ARTEFACT'' verdict is
\emph{confirmed} (not flipped). A positive byproduct: the
WKB closed form $\alpha = A - 2\log c_{b} + \log|c_{a}|$
matches the LSQ-extracted $\alpha$ to $\geq 23$ digits for
all five non-$\Vquad$ families (best $29.79$ digits for
Q$_{L15}$; \Cref{prop:wkb} can be strengthened from the
published $13$-digit floor accordingly). The op:cc-pipeline
problem (\Cref{sec:open}) is therefore split into a Newton-
polygon / Birkhoff-formal-solution sub-problem
(\textsf{op:cc-formal-solution}, the actual numerical
anchor) and the broader Riemann--Hilbert / isomonodromic
matching question (\textsf{op:cc-pipeline} restricted), each
of which is now its own multi-session enterprise.
