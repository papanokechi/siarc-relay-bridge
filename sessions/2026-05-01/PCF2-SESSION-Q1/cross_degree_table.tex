\paragraph{Universal-$\xi_0$ proposition (op:xi0-degree-d, restated).}
For any Z-primitive irreducible $b(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{d}\beta_k n^k$ in
PCF scope (with $a_n=1$), the slope-$(1/d)$ edge of the Newton
polygon of the operator $L_d=1-z\,B_d(\theta+1)-z^2$ at $z=0$ has
characteristic equation $\chi(c)=1-(\beta_d/d^d)\,c^d=0$ with
positive real root
\[
\xi_0(b)\ =\ \frac{d}{\beta_d^{1/d}}.
\]
At $d=2$ this is the proven Prop.~3.3.A of \textsc{channel-theory-v11}.
At $d=4$ this is verified to spread $0$ (relative $<10^{-50}$) across
$8$ representative quartics in Phase Q1-3 of Session Q1. At $d=3$ it
is a clean prediction (no compute performed in this session).

\paragraph{Refined op:d2-anomaly status.}
The $\{2d-1,2d\}$ split phenomenon is now observed only at $d=2$
across the cubic and quartic catalogues. Since the cubic anchor
$+_C3\_\mathrm{real}$ bin contains genuine elementary positives (e.g.\
family 46 $=n^3-2n^2-n+1$) without splitting, and the quartic catalogue
likewise produces no $A=7$ branch, the working interpretation is
\emph{$d=2$ is genuinely anomalous; the natural sharp conjecture for
$d\ge 3$ is $A=2d$.}  This is encoded as the unsplit form of B4 in
PCF-2 v1.1 and not refuted by the present data.

\paragraph{Caveat.}  The lex-window enumeration $a_4\in\{1,2,3,5,7\}$,
$|a_{i<4}|\le 3$ contains no totally imaginary CM quartic.  The
question whether a $-_\mathrm{CM}$ quartic could populate an $A=7$
branch (analogous to $-\_S_3\_\mathrm{CM}$ at $d=3$ being the only
populated cubic bin) is open and is recommended as the next
relay-prompt target.
