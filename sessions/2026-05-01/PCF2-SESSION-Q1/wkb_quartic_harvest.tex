\section*{WKB-quartic harvest (Phase Q1-2)}

\subsection*{Setup}

The PCF-2 v1.1 Conjecture B4 predicts that for every degree-$d$ Z-primitive
irreducible $b(n)$ in scope (with $a_n=1$), the WKB-decay exponent
$A$ satisfies $A=2d$. At $d=2$ Theorem 5 of PCF-1 v1.3 proves the
$\{2d-1,2d\}=\{3,4\}$ split via $\mathrm{sgn}(\Delta_2)$. At $d=3$ Sessions
B and C1 show $A_{\mathrm{fit}}\in[5.88,5.99]$ across all 50 cubic
families (mean $5.973$, $\sigma=0.026$); the elementary-positive
$\{2d-1\}=\{5\}$ branch is empty.

This session tests the conjecture at $d=4$.

\subsection*{Catalogue}

Lex enumeration of $b(n)=a_4 n^4+a_3 n^3+a_2 n^2+a_1 n+a_0$ with
$a_4\in\{1,2,3,5,7\}$, $a_{0,1,2,3}\in\{-3,\ldots,3\}$, $\gcd=1$,
irreducible over $\mathbb{Q}$, kept until 60 entries. Galois-group
distribution:
\[
S_4 : 57,\quad D_4 : 3,\quad V_4 : 0,\quad C_4 : 0,\quad A_4 : 0.
\]
Real-signature distribution:
$(r_1,r_2)=(2,1):59;\ (4,0):1$. The window contains no totally
imaginary or pure-CM quartic; the lex window's $a_4=1,\ a_3=-3$ band
is dominated by mixed-signature $S_4$ extensions.

Calibration anchors (out-of-window): $x^4-2\to D_4$ (Eisenstein at
$2$), $x^4+1\to V_4$ ($\Phi_8$), $x^4-x-1\to S_4$. All three match
expected Galois group.

\subsection*{WKB fit protocol}

For each cataloged family, compute $L_b$ at $\mathrm{dps}=1200$ for
$N\in[10,60]$ step $2$, and fit
\[
\log|L_N - L_{150}|\ \sim\ -A\,N\log N + \alpha\,N - \beta\log N + \gamma
\]
by weighted linear LSQ in $(\log N\cdot N,\,N,\,\log N,\,1)$. Reference
$N_{\mathrm{ref}}=150$ and the $1200$-digit precision floor jointly
guarantee $|L_N - L_{150}|$ exceeds the precision floor for all
$N\in[10,60]$ in 60/60 families.

\subsection*{Results}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Verdict counts}: $\texttt{b4\_consistent\_at\_8}: 60/60$;
        $\texttt{b4\_split\_candidate\_at\_7}: 0$;
        $\texttt{b4\_violating}: 0$;
        $\texttt{STALLED}: 0$.
  \item Empirical $A_{\mathrm{fit}}$: $\overline{A}=7.9544$,
        $\sigma_A=0.0037$, $A_{\min}=7.9488$ (family 1),
        $A_{\max}=7.9605$ (family 60).
  \item All 60 families lie strictly closer to $A=8$ than to $A=7$;
        $|A_{\mathrm{fit}}-8|\le 0.0512$ uniformly.
  \item Stderr range $[0.0021,\,0.0028]$ uniformly across families.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{B4-verdict at $d=4$: SUPPORTED}, $60/60$ families
\texttt{b4\_consistent\_at\_8}.

\subsection*{Systematic offset}

Just as the $d=3$ harvest showed $A_{\mathrm{fit}}$ clustered $\sim 0.025$
below the prediction $A=6$, the $d=4$ harvest shows $A_{\mathrm{fit}}$
clustered $\sim 0.045$ below the prediction $A=8$.  The offset is
consistent with a fit-window bias from the absence of the
$-(\log\log N)$ correction term in the four-parameter ansatz; we did
not extend the ansatz because doing so introduces a fifth parameter
poorly conditioned against $\beta$.  The conclusion --- every family is
within $0.10$ of $A=2d=8$ and strictly farther from $A=2d-1=7$ ---
holds independently.

\subsection*{Bin-driven split: not detected}

Across the 3 distinct quartic bins available in this catalogue
(\texttt{mixed\_$S_4$:57}, \texttt{mixed\_$D_4$:2},
\texttt{$+$\_real\_$D_4$:1}) the $A_{\mathrm{fit}}$ distribution is
unimodal with $\sigma=0.0037$. There is no measurable inter-bin
displacement, in particular no $A=7$ branch. This refines op:d2-anomaly
toward the interpretation \emph{the $\{2d-1,2d\}$ split at $d=2$ is
genuinely special}; cubic ($d=3$) and quartic ($d=4$) both populate
the single branch $A=2d$ within the lex-window catalogue.

This is consistent with Conjecture B4 in its sharp unsplit form
(introduced by Session B):
$A=2d$ for every Z-primitive irreducible $b(n)$ of degree $d\ge 3$ in
scope.  It also strengthens the no-go content of B4$'$ (the conjectured
$\{2d-1\}$ branch is now empirically empty at both $d=3$ and $d=4$).
