## Q-195-2 — Axiom-as-sorry policy

**AXIOMS_DO_NOT_COUNT** (but must be separately reported)

Substantiation (verbatim): Lean-4 distinguishes `axiom` (deliberate, named, surface-visible assumption that survives `#print axioms`) from `sorry` (a placeholder the kernel flags as incomplete and the linter warns on). Conflating them destroys signal: `axiom BSD_weak` in `congruent-relay` is a deliberate design choice tracked in the manuscript's assumptions section, while a `sorry` is an unfulfilled proof obligation. SIARC AEAL discipline already requires axiom-set declarations per claim; counting axioms as sorries would double-charge them and inflate apparent debt. Recommended convention: report as **`(sorries=N, axioms=M)` tuple** in C.3++ messages, never collapsed to a single integer. Risk asymmetry favors this: under-reporting axioms is the failure mode AEAL is designed to catch, and the tuple form forecloses it without sorry-count distortion.
